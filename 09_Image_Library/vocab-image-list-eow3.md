# Vocabulary Image Master List — EOW3

Every EOW3 word that needs a picture. Build each prompt from `style-bible.md` §5,
using the "Pose seeds" below for the `{variant_pose}` slot. Generate `color` + `bw`
for every variant. Update **Status** as you go: `TODO` → `DONE`.

Legend — Type: `obj`=object, `per`=person, `act`=action, `adj`=adjective pair, `col`=color swatch, `set`=setting.
Variants = target number of pose variants.

Face rule (from style-bible §1): only **animals and people** get a cute face. Plants, food,
weather, vehicles, buildings, and all other objects get **NO face**.

Note on de-duplication: EOW3 vocabulary was checked against `09_Image_Library/words/`.
No EOW3 word currently has a folder, so every word is **new** (`TODO`). The word `park`
appears twice with different meanings — Unit 4 *a park* (place) is keyed `park`; Unit 5
*to park* (verb) is keyed `park-car` to avoid a folder collision.

## Grammar / sentence patterns per unit

For later worksheet building. Source: each unit's Official Scope (Grammar 1 / Grammar 2).

| Unit | Theme | Grammar 1 | Grammar 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | The World of Work | Simple present for occupations — *What does your father do? He's a chef. / Where does he work?* | Simple present with *want* + infinitive — *What do you want to be one day? I want to be a movie star.* |
| 2 | Let's Eat! | *any* in questions & negatives — *Are there any tomatoes? No, there aren't any tomatoes.* | Polite requests with *may* — *May I have some chips, please?* |
| 3 | A Helping Hand | Time phrases with *before* / *after* — *She gets dressed before breakfast. / He feeds his bird after school.* | Adverbs of frequency — *never, sometimes, usually, always* |
| 4 | My Place in the World | Ask for help with *can* — *Can you help me? Sure. How can I help?* | Directions: imperative + adverb — *Go straight. Turn left on Third Avenue.* |
| 5 | On the Move! | Agree / disagree with *too* / *not* — *I ride my scooter to school. I do, too. / I don't.* | Contrast with *but* — *My mother takes the bus, but my father takes the subway.* |
| 6 | Our Senses | Linking verbs *taste, smell, look, feel, sound* — *The soup smells great.* | Simple past of *to be* — *How was the ice cream? It was delicious. / How were the cookies?* |
| 7 | Animal Habitats | Cause & effect with *why* / *because* — *Why does a lion have sharp claws? Because it needs to catch its food.* | Infinitives of purpose — *Giraffes use their long tongues to clean their ears.* |
| 8 | What's for Dinner? | Count / noncount with *some* / *any* — *Are there any oranges? Yes, there are some.* | Count / noncount with *a few* / *a little* — *Yes, there are a few. / There is a little.* |
