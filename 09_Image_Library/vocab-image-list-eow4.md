# Vocabulary Image Master List — EOW4

Every EOW4 word that needs a picture. Build each prompt from `style-bible.md` §5,
using the "Pose seeds" below for the `{variant_pose}` slot. Generate `color` + `bw`
for every variant. Update **Status** as you go: `TODO` → `DONE`.

Legend — Type: `obj`=object, `per`=person, `act`=action, `adj`=adjective pair, `col`=color swatch, `set`=setting.
Variants = target number of pose variants. Articles stripped ("a back" → back); multi-word keys hyphenated ("ice cream" → ice-cream).
FACE RULE (from style-bible §1): only animals & people get a cute face; plants, food, sun/moon, weather and all objects get NO face.

EOW4 is more advanced, so several words are abstract or grammar-driven (comparatives, frequency, adjectives like *interesting*). For those the table proposes the clearest concrete depiction and flags them with ⚠ in the pose seed.

## Grammar / sentence patterns per unit (for worksheet building)

| Unit | Theme | Grammar 1 | Grammar 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Feeling Fit | Simple past with *did* + short answers (Did you wash your hands? Yes, I did. / No, I didn't.) | Quantity with *enough* and *too* (It's important to get enough sleep. Don't stay up too late.) |
| 2 | Let's Celebrate! | Past tense of regular verbs: questions & long answers (Did you watch the parade? Yes, I watched the parade.) | Past tense of irregular verbs (Did you go to the parade? Yes, I went. / I saw the fireworks. / I ate cake.) |
| 3 | My Weekend | Simple past with *didn't* in long answers (How was your weekend? It was boring. I didn't do anything special.) | *Go + -ing* in the simple past (We didn't go hiking. We went swimming.) |
| 4 | All in Our Family | Comparatives with *-er* (My best friend is bigger than I am. / Cats are cuter than dogs.) | Future plans with present progressive (What are you doing on Saturday? I'm going to visit my aunt.) |
| 5 | Fresh Food | Obligation with *have to + verb* (I have to wash and cut the vegetables. / She has to plant the tomatoes.) | Order with *would like* (What would you like for lunch? I'd like a bowl of vegetable soup.) |
| 6 | Long Ago and Today | Contrast with *but* and *instead* (Long ago, children walked to school, but I ride my bike.) | Comparatives with *more + adjective* (Life was more difficult back then.) |
| 7 | Get Well Soon! | Give advice with *should* (I have a bad earache. What should I do? You shouldn't wait any longer.) | Reflexive pronouns (Ouch! I cut myself. / Don't hurt yourself. / He shouldn't cook by himself.) |
| 8 | My Favorites | Superlatives with *-est* and *most* (the greatest basketball player / the most amazing writer) | Irregular comparatives & superlatives: better, the best, worse, the worst |

### Hard-to-depict / abstract words flagged (⚠)

Adjectives & abstract concepts that need a clear concrete stand-in: celebrate, dress-up, busy, exciting, interesting, lose, win, cuter, different, friendlier, smarter, grow, every-day, how-often, on-friday, once-a-month, three-times-a-week, twice-a-year, begin, chores, learn, make, spend-time, difficult, expensive, important, modern, old-fashioned, amazing, cool, famous, funny, great, popular, hobby, school-subject, sport. These are best taught with the contrast-pair or icon-plus-symbol approach (e.g. circled item in a pair, calendar markings, star/lightbulb symbols).
