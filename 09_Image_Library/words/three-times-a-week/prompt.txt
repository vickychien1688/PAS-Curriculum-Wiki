# EOW4 Sheet 19b — icon: three-times-a-week

Another image: a neat 2-row by 3-column grid of SIX SEPARATE hand-drawn calendar icons, same soft watercolour / colored-pencil style, NO faces, pure white background, wide clean white gaps between every icon, each fully inside its own cell, ABSOLUTELY NO letters, words or numbers anywhere. Make each one clearly different and easy for a 7-year-old to read. Left to right, top row then bottom row: (1) EVERY DAY: one horizontal week-strip of seven equal day-boxes, EVERY one of the seven boxes stamped with a green check mark. (2) HOW OFTEN: one calendar page, blank except for a single big bold question mark in the middle. (3) ON FRIDAY: one horizontal week-strip of seven day-boxes where the last two boxes on the right (the weekend) are tinted light grey, and the single weekday box immediately before the weekend is highlighted bright red with a red ring around it, all other boxes plain. (4) ONCE A MONTH: one full month grid of about thirty small boxes (clearly a whole month), with exactly ONE box marked by a single big red star and every other box empty. (5) THREE TIMES A WEEK: one horizontal week-strip of seven day-boxes, exactly THREE boxes stamped with a green check mark and the other four plain. (6) TWICE A YEAR: a block of twelve little month pages arranged in 3 rows by 4 columns (clearly a whole year of twelve months), with exactly TWO of the twelve pages marked with a red star and the rest plain.
