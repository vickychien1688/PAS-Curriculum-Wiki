# Vocabulary Image Master List — EOW2

Every EOW2 word that needs a picture. Build each prompt from `style-bible.md` §5,
using the "Pose seeds" below for the `{variant_pose}` slot. Generate `color` + `bw`
for every variant. Update **Status** as you go: `TODO` → `DONE`.

Legend — Type: `obj`=object, `per`=person, `act`=action, `adj`=adjective pair, `col`=color swatch, `set`=setting.
Variants = target number of pose variants (v1 only for now).

**Face rule** (from `style-bible.md`): only animals & people get a face (tiny dot eyes, small smile,
soft rosy cheeks). Plants, food, weather, sun/moon, and all objects get **NO face**.

## Grammar / sentence patterns per unit

For later worksheet building. Images are the focus of this plan; these frames are recorded so worksheets can reuse the library art with the right sentence shells.

| Unit | Theme | Grammar 1 | Grammar 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Animal Friends | Present progressive, 3rd person plural (What are the horses doing? They're running. / Are the sheep sleeping? No, they aren't.) | Want + infinitive, 1st/2nd/3rd person singular (Do you want to ride the goat? / She wants to see the ducks.) |
| 2 | Fun in Class | Present progressive, 1st person plural (What are you doing? We're counting crayons.) | Are there...? / There are / There aren't (Are there any markers on the desk? No, there aren't.) |
| 3 | Boots and Bathing Suits | What's + noun + like? It's... (What's the weather like? It's rainy.) | Imperatives (It's cold. Put on your coat. / Don't forget your umbrella.) |
| 4 | Fun in the Sun | Like + infinitive (Do you like to play baseball? Yes, I do. It's fun. / What do you like to do?) | Let's + verb (Let's throw a ball. OK. What fun!) |
| 5 | Inside Our House | Prepositions: above, behind, in front of, between, next to, under | Subject pronoun agreement with it/they (Where is the phone? It's in the kitchen.) |
| 6 | Day by Day | Telling the time: exact hour (What time is it? It's 1:00. / When do you get up? At 7:00.) | Adverbs of frequency: always, every day, never |
| 7 | How Are You? | How are you? I'm + adjective; describe how a person looks (He looks bored. No, he's tired.) | Regular and irregular plurals (parent/parents, person/people, child/children) |
| 8 | Awesome Animals | Express ability with can/can't (Can a penguin swim? Yes, it can. / Penguins can't fly.) | Simple present, subject-verb agreement (Does a tiger have sharp claws? Yes, it does.) |
