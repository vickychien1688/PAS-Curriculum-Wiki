# Super Teacher Worksheets — Style & Activity Reference

Reference for worksheet **layout conventions** and **activity types**, based on
superteacherworksheets.com samples the teacher provided (Spelling Unit A-1 set).
Use these as templates when building PAS worksheets.

## Visual conventions (the "STW look")

- A thin **rounded rectangle frame** around the whole worksheet body.
- Title in a **rounded "tab"** (pill) at the top-centre of the frame; optional small subtitle line under it.
- **Name:** line at top-left; a small decorative **mascot illustration** in the top-right corner.
- **Handwriting baselines** for early writers: a solid bottom line with a **dashed mid-line** above it (two-line ruling).
- A **Word Box / Word Bank** panel (bordered) holding the target words, usually at the bottom (spelling) or top (ABC order).
- Footer: "Super Teacher Worksheets" + URL. (For PAS: use our own footer + Value line.)
- Mostly **black-and-white**, clean, lots of white space. (PAS can offer colour using `09_Image_Library`.)

## Activity types

### Scrambled Spelling (→ Pattern WS-016, with Word Box)
"Unscramble the spelling words." Numbered list (often 2 columns) of scrambled letters
with two writing lines under each. A **Word Box** of all answers sits at the bottom.

### Scrambled Sentences (→ new Pattern WS-017)
"Unscramble the sentences." Each item gives the words of a sentence out of order
(e.g. `a cat. We have`); the student rewrites the correct sentence on handwriting lines.
Good for grammar / sentence-frame practice (e.g. EOW `It's a ___.`, `Is it a ___?`).

### Word Hunt (→ new Pattern WS-018)
"Circle the words." Each row shows the **target word** on the left, then a short
**string of letters** on the right that contains the word once; the student circles it
(e.g. `hat → a t h a t o h a l t`). Simpler than a full grid word-search; great for Level 1.

### ABC Order (→ new Pattern WS-019)
Word Box at the top; "Write your spelling words in alphabetical order." Two columns of
numbered handwriting lines. **Directly matches EOW1 Unit 1's vocabulary strategy
(alphabetical order).**

### Write It Twice (→ new Pattern WS-020)
"Write each word two times." Numbered list: word printed once, then two **primary-ruled**
(two-line) slots to copy it. The clean copy-practice version of a handwriting page.

### Letter / Colour Recognition (→ new Pattern WS-021)
Pre-K/K. A field of shapes (e.g. apples) each holding a letter; "Colour only the apples
with the letter A a." Adapt for EOW as "colour only the [classroom objects]" or a target letter.

### Sentence Completion (→ new Pattern WS-022)
Word Box at top (e.g. 6 words); numbered sentences each with a blank; student completes
each sentence with a bank word (e.g. `I picked a ____ apple.`). Vocabulary-in-context.

## Mapping to PAS / EOW

| STW type | PAS pattern | Best EOW use |
|---|---|---|
| Scrambled Spelling + Word Box | WS-016 | Any unit's vocabulary spelling recall |
| Scrambled Sentences | WS-017 (new) | Grammar sentence frames (it's, Is it...?, present progressive) |
| Word Hunt | WS-018 (new) | Level 1-2 vocabulary recognition |
| ABC Order | WS-019 (new) | EOW1 U1 (alphabetical order strategy); any spelling list |

Mascot: optional. If used, keep it simple and on-brand; or replace with a `09_Image_Library` icon.
