# Starter PE 2 Teacher's Manual: Day 3 - Dear Zoo

## Overview
- **Theme:** Animals & Pets (It can...)
- **Storybook:** *Dear Zoo* (by Rod Campbell)
- **Duration:** 90 minutes
- **Target Age:** Starter (First-year learners)

## Learning Objectives
- **Vocabulary:** Identify and name animals/pets (bird, cat, dog, fish, monkey, mouse, rabbit, zebra) and size adjectives (big, small).
- **Phonics:** Recognize and pronounce long 'i' word families (-ike, -ime, -ine, -ite, -ide, -ipe, -ire, -ive) from SG3 U3 & U4.
- **Grammar/Sentences:** Describe animals using "It is ___ (big/small)." and "It can ___ (action)."
- **Story Comprehension:** Engage with the story *Dear Zoo* by guessing the hidden animals and discussing why they make good or bad pets.

## Teaching Instructions

### Section 1: Welcome & Song (10 min)

#### Slide 1: Welcome to the Zoo!
- **Objective:** Greet students and set the animal theme.
- **Script:** "Hello, everyone! Today we are going to the zoo! Are you ready to see some animals?"
- **Interaction:** Have students make their favorite animal sound to say hello.

#### Slide 2: Let's Sing!
- **Objective:** Warm up with an animal-themed song.
- **Script:** "Let's sing a song about animals! Stand up and move like the animals!"
- **Interaction:** Play an animal song (e.g., "Let's Go to the Zoo" or "Walking in the Jungle"). Students mimic the animal movements.

### Section 2: Phonics Secret Code (25 min)

#### Slide 3: Today's Secret Sound
- **Objective:** Introduce the long 'i' sound (magic e).
- **Script:** "Today, we have another magic 'e' sound! Look at 'i' and 'e'. The magic 'e' makes 'i' say its name: /aɪ/. Can you say /aɪ/?"
- **Interaction:** Students repeat the sound /aɪ/.

#### Slides 4-6: Word Families (-ike, -ime, -ine, -ite, -ide, -ipe, -ire, -ive)
- **Objective:** Blend sounds to read long vowel words.
- **Script:** "Let's put sounds together! /b/ - /aɪ/ - /k/ ... bike! I can ride a bike."
- **Interaction:** Students blend the sounds and do corresponding actions (pretend to ride a bike, point to the time, hide).
- **Teacher Notes:** Keep the pace lively. Use TPR for words like 'hide' or 'bite'.

#### Slides 7-8: Crack the Code!
- **Objective:** Practice reading long vowel words independently.
- **Script:** "Time for a game! Crack the code. What does k-i-t-e spell? Who knows?"
- **Interaction:** Call on individual students to sound out the word before revealing the picture.

### Section 3: Story Time (20 min)

#### Slide 9: Story Cover - Dear Zoo
- **Objective:** Introduce the story and build anticipation.
- **Script:** "Look at this book! It's called *Dear Zoo*. It's a box from the zoo. What's inside?"
- **Interaction:** Ask students to guess what animal might be in the box.

#### Slides 10-12: Story Scenes
- **Objective:** Read the story, practice guessing, and discuss size/characteristics.
- **Script:** (Read the story text). "They sent me a... Look at the box! It's very heavy. What is it? Let's open it! It's an elephant! He was too big! I sent him back."
- **Interaction:** Before revealing each animal, have students guess based on the shape of the crate/box. Ask: "Is it big or small?" When the animal is revealed, have them shout: "I sent him back!"
- **Teacher Notes:** If you have the lift-the-flap book, let a different student come up to open the flap for each page.

### Section 4: Word Power! (15 min)

#### Slides 13-14: Animal Vocabulary
- **Objective:** Explicitly teach the target vocabulary (bird, cat, dog, fish, monkey, mouse).
- **Script:** "Let's look at some animals. Some can be pets! A bird. A cat. A dog. A fish."
- **Interaction:** Choral repetition. "Say it together: dog! What sound does a dog make? Woof!"

#### Slide 15: The Zoo Box Game
- **Objective:** Kinesthetic vocabulary practice.
- **Script:** "I have a box from the zoo! Who wants to see what's inside? Reach in, pick a card, but don't show us! Make the animal sound or action, and we will guess!"
- **Interaction:** Invite a student to pick an animal card and act it out. The class guesses the animal.

### Section 5: Go Hand in Hand (20 min)

#### Slides 16-17: Sentence Patterns (Description)
- **Objective:** Practice describing animals using size and ability.
- **Script:** "Look at the elephant. Is it big or small? It is big! What can a bird do? It can fly."
- **Interaction:** Teacher shows an animal picture. Students say: "It is [big/small]. It can [action]."

#### Slides 18-19: My Pet
- **Objective:** Connect vocabulary to the theme of pets.
- **Script:** "What is your favorite pet? 'My pet is a dog. It can run.'"
- **Interaction:** Ask a few students: "What is your pet?" Guide them to use the full sentence structure.

#### Slide 20: Worksheet Time
- **Objective:** Consolidate learning through written practice.
- **Script:** "Great job! Now let's do our worksheet. Take out your pencil."
- **Interaction:** Students complete the SP2-03 worksheet.
- **Teacher Notes:** Circulate and assist students. Ensure they are tracing/writing correctly.
