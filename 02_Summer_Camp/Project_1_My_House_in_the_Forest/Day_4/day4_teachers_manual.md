# Teacher's Manual - Day 4: Crafting Our Forest Wheel

## Overview
**Theme:** Project Creation and Presentation
**Time:** 85 minutes
**Learning Objectives:**
1. Students will create a "Brown Bear Forest Wheel" (轉盤書).
2. Students will present their wheel using the target language from the week.
3. Students will practice asking and answering questions about locations.

**Target Vocabulary (Review):**
- Nature: bird, butterfly, tree, river, flower, rock
- Family: mother, father, brother, sister, grandparents
- Rooms: kitchen, bedroom, bathroom, living room, dining room
- Verbs: flying, cooking, sleeping, reading, watching TV, taking a bath

**Target Grammar (Review):**
- "Hello, my name is ___. This is my family. We are in the ___."
- "(Bird), (bird), what do you see? I see a (tree) looking at me!"
- "(Mother), (mother), where are you? I see my (mother) is (cooking)!"
- "Where is (mother)?" -> "She is in the (kitchen)!"

## Slide-by-Slide Script & Instructions

### Section 1: Opening
**Slide 1: Title Slide**
- **Action:** Welcome students with high energy. Point to the Forest Wheel on the screen.
- **Script:** "Good morning, everyone! Welcome to Day 4! Today is the best day because we are making our big project! Look at this amazing Forest Wheel! Are you ready to make one?"

### Section 2: Phonics (SG4 Unit 8)
**Slide 2: Phonics th-**
- **Action:** Demonstrate putting your tongue between your teeth for the 'th' sound.
- **Script:** "Before we make our craft, let's learn some new sounds. Put your tongue between your teeth and blow: 'th-'. T-h-i-n, thin. T-h-i-c-k, thick. T-h-u-m-b, thumb. Your turn! Thin, thick, thumb."

**Slide 3: Phonics -th**
- **Action:** Emphasize the ending 'th' sound.
- **Script:** "The 'th' sound can be at the end too! T-e-e-t-h, teeth. B-a-t-h, bath. C-l-o-t-h, cloth. Let me hear you: teeth, bath, cloth."

**Slide 4: Phonics ph-**
- **Action:** Explain that 'ph' sounds like 'f'.
- **Script:** "This one is tricky! 'ph' sounds like 'f'. P-h-o-t-o, photo. P-h-o-n-e, phone. Ready? Photo, phone."

**Slide 5: Phonics wh-**
- **Action:** Make a blowing 'wh' sound.
- **Script:** "And 'wh-' sounds like 'w'. W-h-a-l-e, whale. W-h-i-t-e, white. Together: whale, white."

**Slide 6: Whiteboard Race!**
- **Action:** Divide the class into teams. Write th-, ph-, wh- on the board.
- **Script:** "Game time! Whiteboard Race! When I say go, run to the board and write a word that starts with th, ph, or wh. Ready? Go!"

### Section 3: Craft Instructions
**Slide 7: Craft Title**
- **Action:** Build excitement for the craft. Show a completed sample if you have one.
- **Script:** "Great job on phonics! Now, it's time to make our Forest Wheel!"

**Slide 8: Step 1 - Two Circles**
- **Action:** Hold up the two blank circle cards.
- **Script:** "Step 1: Everyone needs two circles. One big outer wheel, and one smaller inner wheel."

**Slide 9: Step 2 - Outer Wheel**
- **Action:** Point to the drawings on the outer wheel.
- **Script:** "Step 2: On the big outer wheel, draw your nature scenes and rooms. Draw a tree, a river, a kitchen, a bedroom! Make it colorful!"

**Slide 10: Step 3 - Inner Wheel**
- **Action:** Point to the drawings on the inner wheel.
- **Script:** "Step 3: On the small inner wheel, draw your characters. Mother cooking, bird flying, brother sleeping!"

**Slide 11: Step 4 - Put Together**
- **Action:** Demonstrate using the brass fastener.
- **Script:** "Step 4: Put them together! We will use a special pin in the middle. I will help you with this part."

**Slide 12: Step 5 - Spin and Say!**
- **Action:** Spin your sample wheel.
- **Script:** "Step 5: Spin your wheel! When you see a picture, say the sentence! Now, go back to your desks and let's start crafting! You have 30 minutes."
- *(Teacher Note: Circulate and help students during the 30-minute craft time. Ask them questions as they draw.)*

### Section 4: Presentation Practice
**Slide 13: Show and Tell Time!**
- **Action:** Get students' attention after clean-up.
- **Script:** "Wow, your Forest Wheels look amazing! Now it's Show and Tell Time. You are the stars!"

**Slide 14: Pattern 1 - What do you see?**
- **Action:** Practice the first chant pattern together.
- **Script:** "Let's practice what to say. Spin your wheel to a nature picture. Say: 'Bird, bird, what do you see? I see a tree looking at me!' Let's say it together!"

**Slide 15: Pattern 2 - Where are you?**
- **Action:** Practice the second chant pattern.
- **Script:** "Now spin to a family picture. Say: 'Mother, mother, where are you? I see my mother is cooking!' Try it!"

**Slide 16: Audience Question**
- **Action:** Teach the audience interaction.
- **Script:** "When your friend is presenting, the audience must ask a question! You ask: 'Where is mother?' And the presenter answers: 'She is in the kitchen!' Let's practice!"

**Slide 17: Practice Together!**
- **Action:** Pair students up.
- **Script:** "Practice with your partner! Spin, ask, and answer! I will give you 5 minutes to practice, then we will present to the class!"
- *(Teacher Note: Have students present their wheels to the class or in small groups.)*

### Section 5: Celebration
**Slide 18: We Did It!**
- **Action:** Cheer and applaud the students.
- **Script:** "You did it! Everyone did a fantastic job presenting! Give yourselves a big round of applause!"

**Slide 19: Our Amazing Week**
- **Action:** Briefly review the week's journey.
- **Script:** "Look at our amazing week! On Day 1 we learned about nature. Day 2 was all about family. Day 3 we read our story. And today, you made your own Forest Wheel project! You learned so much!"

**Slide 20: Goodbye!**
- **Action:** Wave goodbye.
- **Script:** "Thank you for a wonderful week in the forest! You are all superstars. Goodbye, everyone!"

## Worksheet Instructions
1. **Page 1 (My Show and Tell Script):** Students use this page to write down their presentation script. Help them fill in the blanks using the Word Bank. They can use this as notes during their presentation.
2. **Page 2 (Phonics th-, ph-, wh-):** Guide students to circle the correct words in Section A, fill in the missing letters in Section B, and draw lines to match in Section C.
3. **Page 3 (My Forest Wheel Plan):** Students use this page *before* making the actual craft. They sketch their ideas for the outer and inner wheels in the blank sections.
4. **Page 4 (My Week Review):** Have students check off the words and sentences they know. They can color the stars to self-assess their learning for the week. Celebrate their progress!
