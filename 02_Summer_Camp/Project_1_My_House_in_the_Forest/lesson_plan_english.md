# Summer Program Project 1: My House in the Forest
## Level 1 - English Lesson Plan (Days 1-4)

This lesson plan covers the 4-day sequence for Project 1, integrating EOW1 Units 2-4, SG2 Phonics, and a story adaptation of *Brown Bear, Brown Bear, What Do You See?* designed for Grade 2 students.

---

### Day 1: Nature Friends
**Theme**: Animals and Nature (EOW1 Unit 2)
**Duration**: 85 minutes

**Learning Objectives**:
Students will be able to identify nature vocabulary and use the present continuous tense to describe what animals are doing.

**Target Vocabulary**:
bird, butterfly, river, rock, sky, sun, tree, bush, cloud, flower, moon, star.
*Word Bank for Review*: fly, look, sit.

**Target Grammar**:
- Present continuous: `What is the bird doing?` `It is looking at me.`
- Location: `Where are the birds?` `They are on the tree.`

#### Lesson Segments

**1. Warm-up & Phonics (25 min)**
- **Phonics Focus**: SG4 Unit 1 (bl-, cl-, fl-) & Unit 2 (br-, cr-, fr-).
- **Activity**: Write the target words on the board (black, clock, flag, brown, crab, frog). Guide students to blend the sounds. Distribute the SG4 Unit 1 & 2 worksheets. Students complete the "Circle the correct answer" and "Match the words" sections.

**2. Lesson Review (15 min)**
- **Vocabulary Booklet**: Introduce the nature vocabulary from Unit 2. Show flashcards of a bird, butterfly, tree, and river.
- **Activity**: Play a quick "Point and Say" game. The teacher says a word, and students point to the correct flashcard and repeat the word.

**3. Grammar Review (25 min)**
- **Grammar into Action**: Introduce the present continuous tense and location prepositions (in, on).
- **Worksheet (10 min)**: Students complete a short worksheet matching animals to their locations (e.g., matching a bird to a tree).
- **Movement Game (15 min)**: "Mime the Action." The teacher calls out an animal and an action (e.g., "Bird, flying!"). Students must mime the action. Then, the teacher asks, "What are you doing?" and students reply, "I am flying!"

**4. Skill Building (20 min)**
- **Story Time**: Read the Day 1 adaptation of *Brown Bear*.
  > "Bird, bird, what do you see? I see a butterfly looking at me."
  > "Butterfly, butterfly, what do you see? I see a flower looking at me."
  > "Flower, flower, what do you see? I see a tree looking at me."
  > "Tree, tree, what do you see? I see a river looking at me."
- **Speaking Drill**: Have students practice the chant in pairs, substituting different nature words from the Word Bank.

### Day 2: Family in the House
**Theme**: Family and Rooms (EOW1 Units 3 & 4)
**Duration**: 85 minutes

**Learning Objectives**:
Students will be able to name family members and rooms in a house, and use question words (Who, Where, What) to ask about their activities.

**Target Vocabulary**:
brother, father, grandfather, grandmother, mother, sister, bathroom, bedroom, dining room, kitchen, living room.
*Word Bank for Review*: cooking, sleeping, watching TV, taking a bath.

**Target Grammar**:
- Question words: `Who`, `Where`, `What`.
- Present progressive: `Where is your mom?` `She is in the kitchen.` `What is she doing?` `She is cooking.`

#### Lesson Segments

**1. Warm-up & Phonics (25 min)**
- **Phonics Focus**: SG4 Unit 3 (gl-, pl-, sl-) & Unit 4 (dr-, gr-, pr-, tr-).
- **Activity**: Introduce the new sounds (glass, play, sled, drum, grape, truck). Distribute the SG4 Unit 3 & 4 worksheets. Students complete the "Circle the correct word" and "Write and say" sections.

**2. Lesson Review (15 min)**
- **Vocabulary Booklet**: Review family members and introduce rooms in the house using a large house poster.
- **Activity**: "Where do they belong?" Give students character cutouts (mother, father, etc.) and have them place the characters in different rooms on the poster.

**3. Grammar Review (25 min)**
- **Grammar into Action**: Focus on forming questions with Who, Where, and What.
- **Worksheet (10 min)**: Students fill in the blanks to complete question sentences based on picture prompts.
- **Movement Game (15 min)**: "Grammar Charades." A student acts out an activity (e.g., sleeping). The class asks, "What are you doing?" The student answers, "I am sleeping." The teacher then asks the class, "Where is he/she?" The class guesses the room (e.g., "In the bedroom!").

**4. Skill Building (20 min)**
- **Story Time**: Read the Day 2 adaptation of *Brown Bear*.
  > "Mother, mother, where are you? I am in the kitchen cooking for you."
  > "Father, father, where are you? I am in the living room watching TV for you."
  > "Brother, brother, where are you? I am in the bedroom sleeping for you."
  > "Sister, sister, where are you? I am in the bathroom taking a bath for you."
- **Speaking Drill**: Students practice the dialogue in pairs, taking turns being the questioner and the family member.

### Day 3: A Happy Forest Home
**Theme**: Reading Comprehension and Integration (EOW1 Units 2-4)
**Duration**: 85 minutes

**Learning Objectives**:
Students will be able to integrate vocabulary from nature, family, and house themes to comprehend a short story and introduce their own family.

**Target Vocabulary**:
Review of all vocabulary from Day 1 and Day 2.
*Word Bank for Review*: big, small, happy, beautiful.

**Target Grammar**:
- Integration of `is/are`, present continuous, and descriptive adjectives.
- `Introduce yourself and your family.`

#### Lesson Segments

**1. Warm-up & Phonics (25 min)**
- **Phonics Focus**: SG4 Unit 5 (sm-, sn-, st-, sw-) & Unit 6 (-nt, -nk, -ng, -ck).
- **Activity**: Practice the sounds (smile, snow, stop, swim, ant, tank, sing, lock). Distribute the SG4 Unit 5 & 6 worksheets. Students complete the "Look and write" and "Listen and Number" sections.

**2. Lesson Review (15 min)**
- **Vocabulary Booklet**: Quick review of the key vocabulary from the past two days using a matching game on the board (match the word to the picture).

**3. Grammar Review (25 min)**
- **Grammar into Action**: Review descriptive adjectives (big, small, happy).
- **Worksheet (10 min)**: Reading comprehension worksheet based on the integrated story. Students answer simple True/False or matching questions.
- **Movement Game (15 min)**: "Brown Bear Room Run." Post room names (kitchen, bedroom, bathroom, living room, dining room) in different corners of the classroom. The game follows the Brown Bear chant structure:
    1. The whole class chants together: "Mother, mother, where are you?"
    2. The teacher continues: "I see my mother is cooking. Where is my mother?"
    3. Students must listen to the action, infer the correct room, and run to that location.
    4. The last student to arrive (or students at the wrong location) is eliminated.
    5. Continue with different family members and actions:
       - "Father, father, where are you? I see my father is watching TV. Where is my father?" → living room
       - "Brother, brother, where are you? I see my brother is sleeping. Where is my brother?" → bedroom
       - "Sister, sister, where are you? I see my sister is taking a bath. Where is my sister?" → bathroom
       - "Grandmother, grandmother, where are you? I see my grandmother is cooking. Where is my grandmother?" → kitchen

**4. Skill Building (20 min)**
- **Story Time**: Read the Day 3 integrated adaptation.
  > "Little bird, little bird, what do you see? I see a big house looking at me."
  > "Big house, big house, what do you see? I see a happy family looking at me."
  > "Happy family, happy family, what do you see? I see a beautiful forest looking at me!"
- **Speaking Drill**: Prepare for the final presentation. Students practice a simple self-introduction script: "Hello, my name is ___. This is my family. We are in the ___."

### Day 4: Crafting Our Forest Wheel
**Theme**: Project Creation and Presentation
**Duration**: 85 minutes

**Learning Objectives**:
Students will create a "Brown Bear Forest Wheel" and present it using the target language from the week.

**Target Vocabulary**:
Review of key vocabulary.

**Target Grammar**:
Review of key sentence patterns for the presentation.

#### Lesson Segments

**1. Warm-up & Phonics (25 min)**
- **Phonics Focus**: SG4 Unit 7 (ch-, sh-) & Unit 8 (th-, ph-, wh-).
- **Activity**: A quick whiteboard game. Practice the sounds (chop, ship, thin, photo, whale). The teacher writes word beginnings (ch-, sh-, th-) and students race to write a complete word. Ensure the academic goal is met before starting the craft.

**2. Craft / Activity (45 min)**
- **Project**: "Brown Bear Forest Wheel" (轉盤書)
- **Instructions (5 min)**: Explain the steps clearly.
  1. Take two pre-cut circle cards (outer wheel and inner wheel).
  2. On the outer wheel: draw or paste pictures in each section — nature scenes (bird, tree, river) and rooms (kitchen, bedroom, bathroom, living room).
  3. On the inner wheel: draw or paste the matching characters/actions (bird flying, mother cooking, father watching TV, brother sleeping, sister taking a bath).
  4. Attach the two circles together with a brass fastener (split pin) in the center.
  5. Spin the wheel! When the window shows a picture, say the Brown Bear sentence.
- **Creation (30 min)**: Students decorate and assemble their wheels. The teacher circulates to help and practices target sentences with students (e.g., "Spin it! What do you see? Where is she?").
- **Practice with Partners (5 min)**: Students pair up and take turns spinning each other's wheels, asking and answering in the Brown Bear pattern.
- **Clean-up (5 min)**: Mandatory clean-up time.

**3. Skill Building with Story (15 min)**
- **Show and Tell**: Students present their Forest Wheels to the class or in small groups.
- **Presentation Format**: Students spin their wheel and use the Brown Bear chant pattern:
  > "(Bird), (bird), what do you see? I see a (tree) looking at me!"
  > "(Mother), (mother), where are you? I see my (mother) is (cooking)!"
- **Interactive Element**: After each presenter spins, the audience asks: "Where is (mother)?" and the presenter answers: "She is in the (kitchen)!"
