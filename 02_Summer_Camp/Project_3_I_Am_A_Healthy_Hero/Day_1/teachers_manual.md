# Day One: Super Body Powers!
## Teacher's Manual & Slide Script

**Project Topic**: I Am a Healthy Hero
**Story Theme**: From Head to Toe (The Healthy Hero Edition - Part 1)
**Time**: 90 minutes
**Target Grammar**: Can / Can't (Ability), Possessive Adjectives (my, his, her)
**Target Vocabulary**: head, eye, ear, nose, mouth, hand, leg, foot, hair, jump, run, walk
**Phonics**: SG6 Unit 1 (-au-, -aw-, -all) & Unit 2 (-oar-, -oor-, -our-)
**Visual Style**: Superhero Comic Style (漫畫格、對話泡泡、動感線條、POW/BAM 效果字)

---

## Slide-by-Slide Script (20 Slides)

### Slide 1: Title Slide
- **Visual**: A bright, comic-book style cover. A superhero emblem with "Healthy Hero" in the center. Pow! and Bam! comic bursts in the background.
- **Teacher Script**: "Good morning, everyone! Welcome to Project Three: I Am a Healthy Hero! Are you ready to be superheroes? Let me hear you say YES!" (Class: YES!)

### Slide 2: Warm-up Song
- **Visual**: Comic panels showing a superhero touching their head, shoulders, knees, and toes.
- **Teacher Script**: "Superheroes need to warm up! Stand up! Let's sing and dance to 'Head, Shoulders, Knees and Toes'."
- **Action**: Play the song and do the actions with the class. Start slow, then do it fast!

### Slide 3: Phonics Introduction
- **Visual**: A superhero holding a magnifying glass. "Phonics Power!" in a speech bubble.
- **Teacher Script**: "Before we learn our super powers, we need Phonics Power! Today we are looking at special letter combinations."

### Slide 4: Phonics — -au- (SG6 Unit 1)
- **Visual**: Comic grid with 4 pictures: sauce, August, Paul, author. Words highlighted with -au- in red.
- **Teacher Script**: "Look at these words. They all have the /ɔː/ sound spelled A-U. Repeat after me: sauce... August... Paul... author. Point to the sauce!"
- **Action**: Have students repeat and point.

### Slide 5: Phonics — -aw- (SG6 Unit 1)
- **Visual**: Comic grid with 4 pictures: paw, draw, yawn, claw. Words highlighted with -aw- in red.
- **Teacher Script**: "Now look at these words. They also have the /ɔː/ sound, but spelled A-W! Repeat: paw... draw... yawn... claw. Show me your claws like a tiger! RRRR!"
- **Action**: Students repeat and do a tiger claw action.

### Slide 6: Phonics — -all (SG6 Unit 1)
- **Visual**: Comic grid with 4 pictures: tall, ball, mall, small. Words highlighted with -all in red.
- **Teacher Script**: "These words end in A-L-L. They say /ɔːl/. Repeat: tall... ball... mall... small. Make yourself very tall! Now make yourself very small!"
- **Action**: Students stretch up high, then crouch down low.

### Slide 7: Phonics — Unit 2 (-oar-, -oor-, -our-)
- **Visual**: A split comic page showing roar, door, four. All have the /ɔːr/ sound.
- **Teacher Script**: "These letters look different, but they sound the same! /ɔːr/. Repeat: roar... door... four. Let me hear your loudest superhero ROAR!"
- **Action**: Class roars together.

### Slide 8: Phonics Game — Sound Stomp
- **Visual**: A big comic boot stomping the ground with "STOMP!" text.
- **Teacher Script**: "Let's play Sound Stomp! I will say a word. If it has the /ɔː/ sound (like sauce, draw, ball), STOMP your foot! If it doesn't, stand still like a statue."
- **Action**: Say words from SG6 Unit 1 & 2 mixed with other random words.

### Slide 9: Vocabulary Introduction (Body Parts)
- **Visual**: A superhero character diagram with labels pointing to body parts (head, eye, ear, nose, mouth, hand, leg, foot, hair).
- **Teacher Script**: "Look at our superhero! What body parts do you see? Touch your head! Touch your nose! Touch your foot!"
- **Action**: Call out body parts rapidly. Students must touch the correct part on themselves.

### Slide 10: Grammar Focus (Can / Can't)
- **Visual**: A superhero jumping over a building (Can) and another trying to fly but falling (Can't).
- **Teacher Script**: "Superheroes CAN do many things. Look! He CAN jump. But he CAN'T fly. What can YOU do? Can you jump? Say: 'I can jump!'"
- **Action**: Have students jump and say the sentence.

### Slide 11: Story Time — The Healthy Hero Edition, Page 1
- **Visual**: Comic panel of the "Super Hero School" gates. Animals in capes are walking in.
- **Text on slide**: "Welcome to the Super Hero School! Every animal has a super power!"
- **Teacher Script**: "It's story time! Look, this is the Super Hero School. Animals go here to learn super powers. Let's see what they can do!"

### Slide 12: Story Time — The Healthy Hero Edition, Page 2
- **Visual**: A gorilla in a cape pounding its chest. "BAM BAM!" comic text.
- **Text on slide**: "I am a gorilla. I pound my chest. Can you do it?"
- **Teacher Script**: "Look at the gorilla! He says, 'I am a gorilla. I pound my chest. Can you do it?' Let's all say: 'I can do it!'"
- **Action**: Class pounds chests and yells "I can do it!"

### Slide 13: Story Time — The Healthy Hero Edition, Page 3
- **Visual**: A giraffe in a mask bending its long neck.
- **Text on slide**: "I am a giraffe. I bend my neck. Can you do it?"
- **Teacher Script**: "Now the giraffe! She says, 'I am a giraffe. I bend my neck. Can you do it?' Answer her!" (Class: "I can do it!")
- **Action**: Class bends necks side to side.

### Slide 14: Story Time — The Healthy Hero Edition, Page 4
- **Visual**: A monkey hanging upside down, waving its arms.
- **Text on slide**: "I am a monkey. I wave my arms. Can you do it?"
- **Teacher Script**: "Here is the monkey. 'I am a monkey. I wave my arms. Can you do it?'" (Class: "I can do it!")
- **Action**: Class waves arms wildly.

### Slide 15: Story Time — The Healthy Hero Edition, Page 5
- **Visual**: An elephant stomping its foot. "STOMP!" comic text.
- **Text on slide**: "I am an elephant. I stamp my foot. Can you do it?"
- **Teacher Script**: "The big elephant! 'I am an elephant. I stamp my foot. Can you do it?'" (Class: "I can do it!")
- **Action**: Class stomps feet loudly.

### Slide 16: Story Time — The Healthy Hero Edition, Page 6
- **Visual**: All the animals looking tired, sweating, and sitting on the ground.
- **Text on slide**: "All the animals are tired. 'We are hungry! We need food!'"
- **Teacher Script**: "Oh no! Using super powers is hard work. Look at the animals. They are tired. They say, 'We are hungry! We need food!' What food will they eat? We will find out tomorrow!"

### Slide 17: Activity Time — Animal Hero Freeze Dance
- **Visual**: Instructions for the Freeze Dance game with music notes and comic action lines.
- **Teacher Script**: "Now it's YOUR turn to be animal heroes! When the music plays, dance! When it stops, I will say a super power like 'Stamp your foot!' You must do the action and freeze!"
- **Action**: Play upbeat music. Stop it and call out actions (Wave your arms! Bend your neck! Pound your chest!).

### Slide 18: Grammar Review (Possessive Adjectives)
- **Visual**: The gorilla pointing to his chest ("his chest"), the giraffe pointing to her neck ("her neck").
- **Teacher Script**: "Before we finish, look here. This is the gorilla. He pounds HIS chest. This is the giraffe. She bends HER neck. Touch YOUR head. Say 'This is MY head!'"

### Slide 19: Worksheet Time
- **Visual**: Preview of Worksheet 1 layout.
- **Teacher Script**: "Superheroes, back to your seats! It's time for our mission worksheet."
- **Action**: Distribute Worksheet 1. Guide students through:
  - Part A: Phonics Word Hunt
  - Part B: Body Parts Matching
  - Part C: Can/Can't Circle

### Slide 20: Wrap-up & Goodbye
- **Visual**: The superhero emblem glowing. "See you tomorrow, Heroes!"
- **Teacher Script**: "You were amazing heroes today! Tomorrow, we will help the animals find good food to get their powers back. See you tomorrow!"
