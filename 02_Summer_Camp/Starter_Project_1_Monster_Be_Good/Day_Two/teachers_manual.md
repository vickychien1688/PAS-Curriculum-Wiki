# Starter Summer Program - Project 1 Day 2
**Theme:** Shapes and Colors
**Storybook:** *Color Zoo* by Lois Ehlert
**Duration:** 90 minutes

---

## 🎯 Learning Objectives
1. **Phonics:** Students can sound out and read CVC words with -ed/-eg/-en/-et (short e) and -ib/-id/-in/-ig (short i).
2. **Vocabulary:** Students can identify and name shapes (circle, square, star, triangle) and colors (red, blue, yellow, green, brown, black).
3. **Sentence Pattern:** Students can ask and answer: "What shape is this?" / "It's a ___." and "What color is this?" / "It's ___."
4. **Listening & Story:** Students can follow the story *Color Zoo* and identify which shapes make animal faces.

## 📖 Teaching Instructions

### Section 1: Welcome & Song (10 min)

#### Slide 1: Welcome
- **Objective:** Greet students, review Day 1, and introduce today's theme.
- **Script:** "Hello everyone! Welcome back to Summer Camp Day 2! Yesterday we learned about classroom objects with Maisy. Today we're going to learn about SHAPES and COLORS! Are you ready?"
- **Interaction:** Students respond "Yes!" and wave.
- **Teacher Notes:** Quickly ask 2-3 students "What did we learn yesterday?" to activate prior knowledge. Accept any answer (book, pencil, bag, etc.).

#### Slide 2: Song Time — "The Shape Song"
- **Objective:** Warm up with a shapes song; pre-teach shape vocabulary through music.
- **Song:** "The Shape Song" by Super Simple Songs
- **Script:** "Let's sing a song about shapes! Stand up everyone! When you hear 'circle,' make a circle with your arms. When you hear 'square,' make a square with your fingers!"
- **Interaction:** Students stand and make shape gestures while singing along.
- **Teacher Notes:** Play the song twice — first time just listen and watch, second time do the actions together. The song covers circle, square, triangle, rectangle. We'll add "star" later.

---

### Section 2: Phonics Secret Code (25 min)

#### Slide 3: Phonics Intro — Short E & Short I
- **Objective:** Introduce today's two vowel sounds.
- **Script:** "Today we have TWO new sounds! First: /ĕ/ — like in 'egg.' Can you say /ĕ/? Good! Second: /ĭ/ — like in 'igloo.' Can you say /ĭ/? Great!"
- **Interaction:** Students repeat /ĕ/ and /ĭ/ several times, exaggerating mouth shape.
- **Teacher Notes:** Point to your mouth. For /ĕ/, mouth is slightly open. For /ĭ/, mouth is more narrow/smiley.

#### Slide 4: Word Family -ed & -eg
- **Objective:** Teach -ed and -eg word families.
- **Script:** "Let's start with -ed. /ĕ/ /d/... -ed! B-ed... bed! R-ed... red! Like the color red! Now -eg. /ĕ/ /g/... -eg! L-eg... leg! Like your leg! P-eg... peg!"
- **Interaction:** Students repeat each word. Point to real objects (a red crayon, their own leg).
- **Teacher Notes:** Connect "red" to yesterday's color knowledge and "leg" to body parts from the Monster project theme. Write words on whiteboard as you say them.

#### Slide 5: Word Family -en & -et
- **Objective:** Teach -en and -et word families.
- **Script:** "Next: -en. /ĕ/ /n/... -en! H-en... hen! P-en... pen! Like your pen for writing! T-en... ten! How many fingers? Ten! Now -et. /ĕ/ /t/... -et! N-et... net! P-et... pet! J-et... jet!"
- **Interaction:** Students repeat. Hold up 10 fingers for "ten." Pretend to catch with a "net."
- **Teacher Notes:** Use TPR (Total Physical Response) — act out each word with gestures.

#### Slide 6: Word Family -ib & -id
- **Objective:** Teach -ib and -id word families.
- **Script:** "Now our /ĭ/ sound! -ib. /ĭ/ /b/... -ib! B-ib... bib! Babies wear a bib! R-ib... rib! Now -id. /ĭ/ /d/... -id! K-id... kid! You are all kids! L-id... lid! The lid on a box."
- **Interaction:** Students repeat. Point to a student: "You're a kid!" Mime opening a lid.
- **Teacher Notes:** "Kid" is a great word for this age group — they love being called "kids" in English.

#### Slide 7: Word Family -in & -ig
- **Objective:** Teach -in and -ig word families.
- **Script:** "Last ones! -in. /ĭ/ /n/... -in! P-in... pin! W-in... win! Who wants to win? B-in... bin! Now -ig. /ĭ/ /g/... -ig! B-ig... big! D-ig... dig! Like digging in the sand! W-ig... wig!"
- **Interaction:** Students repeat. Raise hands for "win!" Make digging motions for "dig."
- **Teacher Notes:** "Big" is especially useful — connect to shapes later ("a BIG circle").

#### Slide 8: Crack the Code Game 1
- **Objective:** Review short e words through a decoding game.
- **Script:** "Time to crack the code! I'll show you letters. Sound them out! Ready? B... E... D. What's the word?"
- **Interaction:** Students sound out letters together and shout the word. Reveal the picture to confirm.
- **Teacher Notes:** Do 4-5 words. Give teams points. Mix -ed, -eg, -en, -et words randomly. Celebrate all correct answers with high-fives.

#### Slide 9: Crack the Code Game 2
- **Objective:** Review short i words through a decoding game.
- **Script:** "More codes to crack! These are /ĭ/ words. Ready? B... I... G. What's the word?"
- **Interaction:** Students decode and shout answers.
- **Teacher Notes:** Do 4-5 words mixing -ib, -id, -in, -ig. Keep energy high. This transitions nicely into story time — "You cracked all the codes! Now let's read a story!"

### Section 3: Story Time (20 min)

#### Slide 10: Story Cover — *Color Zoo*
- **Objective:** Introduce the book and activate curiosity.
- **Script:** "Our story today is called 'Color Zoo'! Look at the cover. What do you see? A tiger! But look carefully — the tiger's face is made of SHAPES! Can you see the circle? Can you see the triangle?"
- **Interaction:** Students point out shapes they see on the cover.
- **Teacher Notes:** Hold up the physical book if available. Let students touch the cover and trace the shapes with their fingers.

#### Slide 11: Story Scene 1 — Tiger (Circle + Square + Triangle)
- **Objective:** Show how shapes combine to make an animal face.
- **Script:** "Look! A tiger! What shapes do you see? A circle for the face... a triangle for the ears... Let's count the shapes together."
- **Interaction:** Students identify and name shapes. "What color is the circle?" "It's orange!"
- **Teacher Notes:** Read the original text slowly. Point to each shape as you name it. Ask "What shape is this?" frequently.

#### Slide 12: Story Scene 2 — Mouse (Circle + Triangle)
- **Objective:** Continue story; show shape transformation.
- **Script:** "Now the tiger is gone! What animal do you see now? A mouse! What shapes make the mouse? The circle is still here, but now we have a different triangle for the ears!"
- **Interaction:** Students compare: "The tiger had big triangles, the mouse has small triangles!"
- **Teacher Notes:** This is the magic of Color Zoo — each page turn removes one shape and reveals a new animal. Emphasize the surprise element.

#### Slide 13: Story Scene 3 — Fox (Square + Triangle)
- **Objective:** Continue story; introduce square shape in context.
- **Script:** "Oh! Now what? A fox! The circle is gone. What shapes are left? A square and a triangle! The fox has a square face!"
- **Interaction:** Students shout out shapes and colors they see.
- **Teacher Notes:** Ask prediction questions: "If I take away the triangle, what animal will we see next?"

#### Slide 14: Interactive — "Make an Animal Face!"
- **Objective:** Students apply shape knowledge creatively.
- **Script:** "Now it's YOUR turn! If you had a circle, a square, and a triangle, what animal would YOU make? Let's try together! I'll put shapes on the board — you tell me what animal it looks like!"
- **Interaction:** Teacher arranges magnetic shapes on the whiteboard. Students guess the animal.
- **Teacher Notes:** Prepare 3-4 combinations. Let 2-3 students come up and arrange shapes themselves. This is a great transition to vocabulary — "What SHAPE is this? What COLOR is this?"

---

### Section 4: Word Power! (15 min)

#### Slide 15: Shapes Vocabulary
- **Objective:** Formally teach/review the 4 target shapes.
- **Script:** "Let's learn our shapes! This is a CIRCLE. Everyone say 'circle'! This is a SQUARE. Say 'square'! This is a TRIANGLE. Say 'triangle'! This is a STAR. Say 'star'!"
- **Interaction:** Students repeat each word 3 times. Draw each shape in the air with their finger.
- **Teacher Notes:** Use flashcards. After introducing all 4, do a quick "point to" game: "Point to the circle! Point to the star!" Speed up gradually.

#### Slide 16: Colors Vocabulary
- **Objective:** Formally teach/review the 6 target colors.
- **Script:** "Now colors! RED! Like a fire truck. BLUE! Like the sky. YELLOW! Like the sun. GREEN! Like grass. BROWN! Like chocolate. BLACK! Like night time."
- **Interaction:** Students repeat. Look around the room: "Can you find something RED? Point to it!"
- **Teacher Notes:** Use color flashcards or point to real objects in the classroom. The "find something [color]" game gets students moving and engaged.

#### Slide 17: Shape + Color Combinations
- **Objective:** Combine shapes and colors in phrases.
- **Script:** "Now let's put them together! What's this? It's a RED circle! What's this? It's a BLUE square! What's this? It's a YELLOW star!"
- **Interaction:** Students say the full phrase: "It's a [color] [shape]."
- **Teacher Notes:** Hold up colored shape cutouts. This prepares them for the sentence pattern practice.

#### Slide 18: Mystery Box Game
- **Objective:** Practice identifying shapes by touch.
- **Script:** "Time for the Mystery Box! I have shapes inside. Who wants to try? Reach in... feel it... DON'T look! What shape is it?"
- **Interaction:** Student reaches in, feels the shape, and guesses: "It's a... triangle!" Then pulls it out to check.
- **Teacher Notes:** Put foam/paper shape cutouts in the box. After the student pulls it out, ask the class: "What COLOR is it?" Student answers: "It's a green triangle!" Do 5-6 rounds.

---

### Section 5: Go Hand in Hand (20 min)

#### Slide 19: Sentence Pattern — "What shape is this?"
- **Objective:** Practice the Q&A pattern with shapes.
- **Script:** "Let's practice our question! I ask: 'What shape is this?' You answer: 'It's a ___.' Ready? (holds up circle) What shape is this?"
- **Interaction:** Students answer: "It's a circle!" Repeat with all 4 shapes.
- **Teacher Notes:** First do teacher-to-class, then pair students up. Walk around and listen. Give stickers to pairs who do it well.

#### Slide 20: Sentence Pattern — "What color is this?"
- **Objective:** Practice the Q&A pattern with colors.
- **Script:** "Now colors! I ask: 'What color is this?' You answer: 'It's ___.' (holds up red card) What color is this?"
- **Interaction:** Students answer: "It's red!" Then combine: "What color is this triangle?" → "It's a blue triangle!"
- **Teacher Notes:** Increase difficulty by combining shape + color. Use the colored shape cutouts from the Mystery Box.

#### Slide 21: Worksheet Time & Goodbye
- **Objective:** Complete the Day 2 phonics worksheet; stamp passports.
- **Script:** "Great job today! Now let's do our worksheet. Look at Section A — match the pictures to the words. Section B — circle the correct word. I'll come around to help you!"
- **Interaction:** Students work independently on worksheets. Teacher circulates and assists.
- **Teacher Notes:**
  - Hand out worksheets.
  - Walk around to help with phonics section (some students may struggle with -eg vs -ig).
  - When students finish, stamp their Learning Passport for Day 2.
  - Closing: "Tomorrow we're going to meet a MONSTER! See you next time! Bye bye!"

## 🎯 Assessment Notes

**Observe during class:**
- Can students distinguish /ĕ/ from /ĭ/ sounds?
- Can students name all 4 shapes without prompting?
- Can students say "It's a [color] [shape]" fluently?
- Are students participating in the Mystery Box and story activities?

**Feedback approach:**
- Use encouraging language: "Great try!", "Almost!", "Yes! It's a triangle!"
- Award stickers for participation, not just accuracy.
- No red pen — use stars and smiley faces on worksheets.
