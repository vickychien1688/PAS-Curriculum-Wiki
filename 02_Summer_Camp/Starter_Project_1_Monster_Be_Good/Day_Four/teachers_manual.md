# Teacher's Manual - Starter PE 1: Monster, Be Good! (Day 4)

## 📌 Project Overview
- **Project Name:** Monster, Be Good!
- **Day:** 4 (Craft Day & Show and Tell)
- **Duration:** 90 minutes
- **Theme:** Monster Review & Puppet Making

## 🎯 Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Review and identify SG2 U1-U6 short vowel CVC words.
2. Follow multi-step instructions to create a "Shape-O-Monster" puppet.
3. Confidently present their craft using target sentences: "I have a monster. It has [number] [body parts]. It's a [shape]."
4. Actively participate in the final story reading of *Monster, Be Good!* using their puppets.

## 🛠 Materials Needed
- Day 4 Presentation Slides
- Craft materials: Colored paper shapes (squares, circles, triangles), googly eyes (optional), glue sticks, scissors, popsicle sticks or paper bags (for the puppet base).
- "Good Monster" star stickers.
- The storybook: *Monster, Be Good!* (Natalie Marshall)
- Optional: Small "Stop" signs for students to wave during the story.

---

## ⏱ Lesson Flow (90 Minutes)

### 1. Warm-up & Song Review (10 min)
**Goal:** Energize the students and review songs from Day 1-3.
- **Action:** Play a medley of the three songs learned this week:
  1. *School Supplies Song* (Day 1)
  2. *The Shape Song* (Day 2)
  3. *Go Away, Big Green Monster!* (Day 3)
- **Teacher Script:** 
  > "Hello, everyone! Are you ready to sing and dance? Let's review all our favorite songs from this week!"

### 2. Phonics Review Game (20 min)
**Goal:** Review SG2 U1-U6 word families without introducing new ones.
- **Action:** Use the presentation slides to play a fast-paced review game (e.g., "Splat the Word" or "Monster Code Breaker").
- **Content:** Mix of words from -ad, -am, -ap, -ag, -an, -at, -ed, -eg, -en, -et, -ib, -id, -in, -ig, -ip, -it, -ix, -og, -op, -ot, -ox.
- **Teacher Script:** 
  > "Look at the monster's belly! What word is it? Can you read it fast?"

### 3. Craft Instructions: Shape-O-Monster (10 min)
**Goal:** Explain the craft process clearly using visual aids.
- **Action:** Show the step-by-step slides. Pair students into groups if needed.
- **Steps to explain:**
  1. Choose a head/body shape (Square, Circle, or Triangle).
  2. Add unusual numbers of body parts (e.g., 3 eyes, 4 arms).
  3. Glue a "Good Monster" star on the tummy.
- **Teacher Script:** 
  > "Today, we are making our very own Shape-O-Monster! First, choose a shape. Then, give it some eyes, arms, and legs. Make it silly!"

### 4. Craft Time (30 min)
**Goal:** Students create their puppets.
- **Action:** Distribute materials. Walk around the classroom and encourage students to use English while creating.
- **Teacher Script:** 
  > "Do you want a circle or a square? How many eyes does your monster have?"
- **Clean-up (5 min):** Ensure the last 5 minutes are dedicated to clearing desks.

### 5. Show and Tell Presentation (15 min)
**Goal:** Students present their monsters using target sentences.
- **Action:** Have students come up to the front (or use a cardboard "stage"). Use the slide with sentence frames to help them.
- **Target Sentences:** 
  - "I have a monster."
  - "It has [3] [eyes]."
  - "This is my head. It's a [square]."
- **Teacher Script:** 
  > "Who wants to show their monster? Use a loud, clear voice!"

### 6. Story Integration: Monster, Be Good! (5 min)
**Goal:** Final interactive reading using the newly made puppets.
- **Action:** Read *Monster, Be Good!* aloud. Whenever the monster in the book misbehaves, students hold up their puppets and shout the phrase.
- **Teacher Script:** 
  > "Listen to the story. Oh no, the monster is eating too many cookies! What do we say? Ready? 1, 2, 3... MONSTER, BE GOOD!"
