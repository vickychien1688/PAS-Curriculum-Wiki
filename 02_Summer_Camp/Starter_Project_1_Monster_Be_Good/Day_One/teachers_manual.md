# Starter PE 1 Teacher's Manual: Day 1 - Classroom Objects

## Overview
- **Theme:** Classroom Objects & Greetings
- **Storybook:** *Maisy Goes to Preschool* (by Lucy Cousins)
- **Duration:** 90 minutes
- **Target Age:** Starter (First-year learners)

## Learning Objectives
- **Vocabulary:** Identify and name classroom objects (bag, book, crayon, pencil, board, chair, globe, table).
- **Phonics:** Recognize and pronounce short 'a' word families (-ad, -am, -ap, -ag, -an, -at).
- **Grammar/Sentences:** Ask and answer questions about objects using "What's this? It's a ___." and "Is it a ___? Yes, it is. / No, it isn't."
- **Story Comprehension:** Engage with the story *Maisy Goes to Preschool* and connect it to classroom vocabulary.

## Teaching Instructions

### Section 1: Welcome & Song (10 min)

#### Slide 1: Welcome to Summer Camp!
- **Objective:** Set a positive tone and introduce the day's theme.
- **Script:** "Hello, everyone! Welcome to our Summer Camp! I am Teacher [Name]. What's your name? Let's say hello to our friends!"
- **Interaction:** Have students turn to a partner and practice "Hello, I am [Name]."
- **Teacher Notes:** Keep the energy high. Make sure every student gets a chance to say their name.

#### Slide 2: Let's Sing!
- **Objective:** Warm up with a song related to classroom objects.
- **Script:** "Are you ready to sing? Let's sing the School Supplies Song! Stand up and show me your hands!"
- **Interaction:** Play "School Supplies Song" (by Fun Kids English). Students mimic the teacher's actions (pretending to write with a pencil, open a book, put things in a bag, etc.).
- **Teacher Notes:** Encourage big movements. This sets the stage for the vocabulary later.

### Section 2: Phonics Secret Code (25 min)

#### Slide 3: Today's Secret Sound
- **Objective:** Introduce the short 'a' sound.
- **Script:** "Today, we have a secret sound! Look at this big letter. What letter is it? Yes, 'a'! And 'a' says /æ/. Can you say /æ/?"
- **Interaction:** Students repeat the sound /æ/ loudly and softly.

#### Slides 4-6: Word Families (-ad, -am, -ap, -ag, -an, -at)
- **Objective:** Blend sounds to read CVC words.
- **Script:** "Let's put sounds together! /s/ - /æ/ - /d/ ... sad! Show me a sad face."
- **Interaction:** Students blend the sounds with the teacher and do the corresponding actions (sad face, act like a dad, act mad).
- **Teacher Notes:** Go through the words briskly. Focus on the blending process.

#### Slides 7-8: Crack the Code!
- **Objective:** Practice reading CVC words independently.
- **Script:** "Time for a game! Crack the code. What does c-a-t spell? Who knows?"
- **Interaction:** Call on individual students to sound out the word before revealing the picture.

### Section 3: Story Time (20 min)

#### Slide 9: Story Cover - Maisy Goes to Preschool
- **Objective:** Introduce the story and build anticipation.
- **Script:** "Look at this book! It's called *Maisy Goes to Preschool*. Who is this? Yes, it's a mouse! Her name is Maisy. Let's see what she does at school."
- **Interaction:** Ask students what they see on the cover. "What is Maisy holding? A paintbrush!"

#### Slides 10-12: Story Scenes
- **Objective:** Read the story and connect it to classroom vocabulary.
- **Script:** (Read the story text or summarize the scenes). "Look! Maisy is painting. What color is the paint? Red! What is this? It's a chair."
- **Interaction:** Pause frequently to ask questions. "Can you find the book? Point to the book!"
- **Teacher Notes:** Use exaggerated voices for different characters. If you have the physical book, hold it up alongside the slides.

### Section 4: Word Power! (15 min)

#### Slides 13-14: Classroom Objects
- **Objective:** Explicitly teach the target vocabulary.
- **Script:** "Maisy has many things in her classroom. What do we have? Look! A bag. A book. A crayon. A pencil."
- **Interaction:** Choral repetition. "Say it together: bag! Show me your bag!" (Students touch their actual bags).
- **Teacher Notes:** Use realia (real objects) in the classroom to reinforce the words.

#### Slide 15: Mystery Box Game
- **Objective:** Kinesthetic vocabulary practice.
- **Script:** "I have a Mystery Box! What's inside? Reach in, feel it, but don't look! Is it a pencil? Is it a crayon?"
- **Interaction:** Invite a student to the front, reach into the box, and guess the object before pulling it out.
- **Teacher Notes:** Fill the box with a real pencil, crayon, small book, and toy chair.

### Section 5: Go Hand in Hand (20 min)

#### Slides 16-17: Sentence Patterns
- **Objective:** Practice asking and answering questions.
- **Script:** "Let's ask a question! 'What's this?' (Teacher points to a picture). You say, 'It's a book.'"
- **Interaction:** Teacher asks, students answer. Then, split the class: Group A asks "What's this?", Group B answers.

#### Slides 18-19: Yes/No Questions
- **Objective:** Practice Yes/No questions.
- **Script:** "(Holding a pencil) Is it a table? No, it isn't! Is it a pencil? Yes, it is!"
- **Interaction:** Teacher holds up objects and asks trick questions. Students shout "Yes, it is!" or "No, it isn't!"

#### Slide 20: Worksheet Time
- **Objective:** Consolidate learning through written practice.
- **Script:** "Great job! Now let's do our worksheet. Take out your pencil."
- **Interaction:** Students complete the SPI-01 worksheet.
- **Teacher Notes:** Circulate and assist students. Ensure they are tracing/writing correctly.
