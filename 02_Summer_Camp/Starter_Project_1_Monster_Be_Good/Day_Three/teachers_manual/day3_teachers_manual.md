# Starter PE 3 — Day 3 Teacher's Manual

**Theme:** My Monster (Body Parts)
**Storybook:** *Monster, Be Good!* by Natalie Marshall
**Duration:** 90 minutes

---

## Learning Objectives
1. **Phonics:** Students can sound out and read CVC words with -ip/-it/-ix (short i) and -og/-op/-ot/-ox (short o).
2. **Vocabulary:** Students can identify and name body parts (eye, ear, nose, mouth, arm, leg).
3. **Sentence Pattern:** Students can say: "I have a monster." / "It has ___ eyes." / "This is my head." / "These are my legs."
4. **Listening & Story:** Students can follow the story *Monster, Be Good!* and respond with the phrase "Monster, be good!" when the monster misbehaves.

## Teaching Instructions

### Section 1: Welcome & Song (10 min)

#### Slide 1: Welcome
- **Objective:** Greet students, review Days 1-2, and introduce today's theme.
- **Script:** "Hello everyone! Welcome back to Summer Camp Day 3! We've learned about classroom objects and shapes. Today we're going to learn about BODY PARTS and make a MONSTER! Are you ready?"
- **Interaction:** Students respond "Yes!" and do a monster pose (hands up like claws).
- **Teacher Notes:** Quickly review: "What shapes did we learn yesterday?" (circle, square, triangle, star). "What colors?" (red, blue, green...). "Great! Today we need those shapes and colors to build a monster!"

#### Slide 2: Song Time — "Go Away, Big Green Monster!"
- **Objective:** Energize students and pre-teach body part vocabulary through music.
- **Script:** "Let's sing our monster song! When you hear a body part, POINT to it on your face!"
- **Actions:**
  1. Play the song video (or sing along with slides).
  2. First time: Teacher demonstrates pointing to body parts as they're mentioned.
  3. Second time: Students join in pointing and singing.
- **Key lyrics to highlight:** "two big yellow EYES" (point to eyes), "a long bluish-greenish NOSE" (point to nose), "a big red MOUTH" (point to mouth), "two little squiggly EARS" (point to ears).
- **Teacher Notes:** This song naturally introduces the body parts vocabulary that will be formally taught later. Don't worry about students knowing all the words yet — the goal is exposure and fun.

---

### Section 2: Phonics Secret Code (25 min)

#### Slide 3: Phonics Intro — Short I & Short O
- **Objective:** Introduce today's vowel sounds.
- **Script:** "Today we have TWO new sounds! The first one is /ĭ/ — like in 'it' and 'is'. The second one is /ŏ/ — like in 'on' and 'octopus'. Let's practice! Say /ĭ/... /ŏ/..."
- **Interaction:** Students repeat the sounds. Teacher shows mouth position.
- **Note:** Do NOT write phonetic symbols on the slide. Just say the sounds orally.

#### Slide 4: Word Family -ip
- **Objective:** Teach -ip words with illustrations.
- **Script:** "Our first word family is -ip! Look — lip, rip, hip, sip. The 'ip' part sounds the same in every word! Let's read them together."
- **Interaction:** Point to each picture. Students repeat each word. Emphasize the colored -ip ending.
- **Words:** lip (picture of lips), rip (picture of paper being ripped), hip (picture of someone pointing to hip), sip (picture of someone sipping a drink)

#### Slide 5: Word Family -it
- **Objective:** Teach -it words with illustrations.
- **Script:** "Next family is -it! Sit, fit, hit, pit. Can you hear the /ĭt/ sound?"
- **Interaction:** Students repeat. Do actions: "sit" (sit down), "hit" (pretend to hit a ball).
- **Words:** sit (picture of child sitting), fit (picture of puzzle piece fitting), hit (picture of bat hitting ball), pit (picture of a pit/hole)

#### Slide 6: Word Family -ix
- **Objective:** Teach -ix words with illustrations.
- **Script:** "Last short-i family is -ix! Six, mix, fix. How many is six? Show me six fingers!"
- **Interaction:** Students hold up 6 fingers. Pretend to mix something. Pretend to fix something.
- **Words:** six (picture of number 6), mix (picture of mixing bowl), fix (picture of fixing with tools)

#### Slide 7: Word Family -og & -op
- **Objective:** Teach -og and -op words with illustrations.
- **Script:** "Now let's learn short O! First family: -og! Dog, log, fog, jog. Second family: -op! Mop, hop, cop, top."
- **Interaction:** Students repeat. Do actions: "jog" (jog in place), "hop" (hop once).
- **Words:** dog, log, fog, jog / mop, hop, cop, top (each with illustration)

#### Slide 8: Word Family -ot & -ox
- **Objective:** Teach -ot and -ox words with illustrations.
- **Script:** "Next: -ot! Pot, hot, cot, not. And -ox! Box, ox, fox."
- **Interaction:** Students repeat. "Is the pot hot? Yes! Don't touch!" (pretend to touch something hot). "What's in the box?" (mystery gesture).
- **Words:** pot, hot, cot, not / box, ox, fox (each with illustration)

#### Slide 9: Phonics Review Game — Monster Word Hunt
- **Objective:** Review all words learned in a game format.
- **Script:** "Let's play Monster Word Hunt! I'll show you a picture. You tell me the word! Ready?"
- **Interaction:** Show pictures one by one. Students shout out the word. Give points to teams.
- **Teacher Notes:** Use 6-8 pictures from today's word families. Make it fast-paced and exciting.

#### Slide 10: Crack the Code — Monster Words
- **Objective:** Blend sounds to make words related to today's theme.
- **Script:** "Can you crack the monster code? What word do these letters make? D-O-G... DOG! F-O-X... FOX! Let's see if you can get them all!"
- **Interaction:** Students blend sounds together. Reveal the picture after they guess correctly.
- **Teacher Notes:** Use words that connect to the monster theme where possible (e.g., "big" from -ig yesterday, "fox" from -ox today).

### Section 3: Story Time (20 min)

#### Slide 11: Story Cover — Monster, Be Good!
- **Objective:** Introduce the storybook and build excitement.
- **Script:** "It's STORY TIME! Today's story is called... 'Monster, Be Good!' Look at this monster! What do you see? Does he look nice or naughty?"
- **Interaction:** Students describe what they see. Accept any language (big, blue, scary, funny, etc.).
- **Teacher Notes:** Hold up the physical book if available. Let students touch the cover.

#### Slide 12: Story Scene 1 — Monster at Home
- **Objective:** Read the first part of the story, establish the interactive response.
- **Script:** "The monster is at home. He's eating cookies... TOO MANY cookies! Oh no! What should we say? 'MONSTER, BE GOOD!'"
- **Interaction:** Every time the monster misbehaves, students shout "MONSTER, BE GOOD!" together.
- **Teacher Notes:** Read with exaggerated expressions. Make the monster's voice deep and silly. Pause before each misbehavior to build anticipation.

#### Slide 13: Story Scene 2 — Monster Making a Mess
- **Objective:** Continue the story, reinforce the interactive phrase.
- **Script:** "Now the monster is making a mess! He's throwing things everywhere! What do we say?"
- **Interaction:** Students shout "MONSTER, BE GOOD!" Use body language — shake finger at the monster.
- **Teacher Notes:** Point to body parts as they appear in the story: "Look at his ARMS! He's using his ARMS to throw things!"

#### Slide 14: Story Scene 3 — Monster Being Good
- **Objective:** Conclude the story with a positive resolution.
- **Script:** "The monster heard you! He's being good now! He's cleaning up. Good monster! Let's give him a clap!"
- **Interaction:** Students clap for the monster. Ask: "What body parts did the monster use to clean up?" (arms, legs)

#### Slide 15: Story Interactive — Build a Monster
- **Objective:** Connect story to vocabulary by describing monster body parts.
- **Script:** "Let's look at our monster friend! How many eyes does he have? How many arms? How many legs? Let's count together!"
- **Interaction:** Count body parts together. Students use fingers to show numbers.
- **Teacher Notes:** This slide transitions into the Word Power section by focusing on body parts.

---

### Section 4: Word Power! (15 min)

#### Slide 16: Body Parts — Face (eye, ear, nose, mouth)
- **Objective:** Teach face-related body parts vocabulary.
- **Script:** "Let's learn the body parts! Touch your EYES! Touch your EARS! Touch your NOSE! Touch your MOUTH!"
- **Interaction:** TPR (Total Physical Response) — students touch each body part as teacher says it. Repeat 3 times, getting faster each time.
- **Teacher Notes:** Use the monster from the story: "The monster has 3 eyes! But you have... 2 eyes! Touch your eyes!"

#### Slide 17: Body Parts — Body (arm, leg)
- **Objective:** Teach body-related vocabulary.
- **Script:** "Now let's learn more! These are your ARMS! Wave your arms! These are your LEGS! Stomp your legs!"
- **Interaction:** Students wave arms, stomp legs. "How many arms do you have? TWO! How many legs? TWO!"
- **Teacher Notes:** Compare to the monster: "The monster has 4 arms! You have 2 arms. The monster has 3 legs! You have 2 legs."

#### Slide 18: Body Parts Review — Simon Says
- **Objective:** Review all 6 body parts through a game.
- **Script:** "Let's play Simon Says! Simon says... touch your nose! Simon says... wave your arms! Touch your ears! — Oops! I didn't say Simon Says!"
- **Interaction:** Play 2-3 rounds of Simon Says using all 6 body parts.
- **Teacher Notes:** Start slow, then speed up. Students who make mistakes sit down. Last one standing wins a sticker.

#### Slide 19: Mystery Box — Body Parts
- **Objective:** Practice vocabulary through tactile guessing game.
- **Script:** "Time for Mystery Box! I have body parts inside. Reach in, feel it, and tell me — what body part is it?"
- **Interaction:** Students reach into the box (containing foam/paper body part cutouts) and guess which body part they're touching.
- **Teacher Notes:** Use exaggerated foam cutouts of eyes, ears, nose, mouth, arm, leg. Students say "It's a/an ___!" when they guess.

---

### Section 5: Go Hand in Hand (20 min)

#### Slide 20: Sentence Pattern — "I have a monster."
- **Objective:** Teach the target sentence patterns.
- **Script:** "Now let's talk about OUR monsters! Say after me: 'I have a monster.' 'It has 3 eyes.' 'It has 4 arms.' 'This is my head.' 'These are my legs.'"
- **Interaction:** Students repeat each sentence. Then practice with different numbers: "It has ___ eyes."
- **Teacher Notes:** Use flashcards or point to the slide. Students fill in different numbers.

#### Slide 21: Sentence Practice — Describe the Monster
- **Objective:** Students practice describing a monster using target sentences.
- **Script:** "Look at this monster! Let's describe it together. How many eyes? 'It has 2 eyes.' What color? 'It's green.' How many arms? 'It has 4 arms.'"
- **Interaction:** Teacher points to different parts of the monster. Students say the sentence.
- **Teacher Notes:** Call on individual students to describe one part each. Encourage full sentences.

#### Slide 22: Worksheet & Goodbye
- **Objective:** Complete the day's worksheet and preview tomorrow's craft.
- **Script:** "Great job today! Now let's do our worksheet. Look at the monster pictures and circle the correct body part words!"
- **Interaction:** Students complete worksheet SPI-03 (circle correct body part words + write "My monster has ___ ___.")
- **Closing Script:** "Tomorrow is CRAFT DAY! We're going to make our OWN monster puppet! Think about what YOUR monster will look like. How many eyes? What color? See you tomorrow!"
- **Teacher Notes:** Stamp passports. Hand out worksheets. Play the monster song softly in the background while students work.

**"Go Away, Big Green Monster!"**

- Connection to story: Both involve telling a monster what to do ("Go away!" / "Be good!")
