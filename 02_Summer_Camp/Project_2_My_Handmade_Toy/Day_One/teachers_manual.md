# Day One: In the Toy Store
## Teacher's Manual & Slide Script

**Project Topic**: My Handmade Toy
**Story Theme**: Corduroy (Part 1)
**Time**: 85 minutes
**Target Grammar**: Demonstratives (This/That/These/Those), Possessives (my/his/her)
**Target Vocabulary**: ball, car, doll, robot, teddy bear, train
**Phonics**: SG5 Unit 1 (-ai-, -ay-) & Unit 2 (-ea-, -ee-)

---

## Slide-by-Slide Script (18 Slides)

### Slide 1: Title Slide
- **Visual**: Warm watercolor illustration of a vintage toy store exterior.
- **Teacher Script**: "Good morning, everyone! Welcome to Project Two: My Handmade Toy. Today is Day One, and we are going to a very special place. Look at the picture! Where are we?" (Wait for answers) "Yes! A toy store!"

### Slide 2: Phonics Introduction
- **Visual**: Title card "Sounds Great! Long A and Long E" with warm watercolor border.
- **Teacher Script**: "Before we go inside the toy store, let's warm up our voices! Today we are learning about Long A sounds and Long E sounds."

### Slide 3: Phonics — -ai- (SG5 Unit 1)
- **Visual**: 4 watercolor pictures in 2x2 grid: mail, wait, train, rain. Each word highlighted with -ai- in color.
- **Teacher Script**: "Look at these words. They all have the /eɪ/ sound spelled A-I. Repeat after me: mail... wait... train... rain. Can you hear the /eɪ/ sound? Point to the train!"
- **Activity**: Have students repeat each word 2-3 times. Ask: "What sound do you hear in the middle?"

### Slide 4: Phonics — -ay- (SG5 Unit 1)
- **Visual**: 4 watercolor pictures in 2x2 grid: day, pay, gray, crayon. Each word highlighted with -ay- in color.
- **Teacher Script**: "Now look at these words. They also have the /eɪ/ sound, but spelled A-Y at the end! Repeat: day... pay... gray... crayon."
- **Activity**: "What color is the crayon? It's gray! Can you find something gray in our classroom?"

### Slide 5: Phonics — -ea- (SG5 Unit 2)
- **Visual**: 4 watercolor pictures in 2x2 grid: sea, meat, beach, seat. Each word highlighted with -ea- in color.
- **Teacher Script**: "New sound! /iː/ spelled E-A. Repeat: sea... meat... beach... seat. Close your eyes and imagine the beach!"
- **Activity**: Students repeat and do a gesture for each word (wave for sea, eat for meat, etc.)

### Slide 6: Phonics — -ee- (SG5 Unit 2)
- **Visual**: 4 watercolor pictures in 2x2 grid: feet, teeth, tree, green. Each word highlighted with -ee- in color.
- **Teacher Script**: "Same /iː/ sound, but spelled E-E! Repeat: feet... teeth... tree... green. Point to your teeth! Point to your feet!"
- **Activity**: "What color is the tree? Green! Both words have /iː/!"

### Slide 7: Phonics Review Game
- **Visual**: All 8 phonics words mixed up with pictures. A game-style layout.
- **Teacher Script**: "Let's play a game! I will say a word. You point to the picture and tell me: is it A-I, A-Y, E-A, or E-E?"
- **Activity**: Say words in random order. Students point and identify the spelling pattern. Award points for correct answers.

### Slide 8: Vocabulary Introduction (Toys)
- **Visual**: Six watercolor toys on wooden shelves: ball, car, doll, robot, teddy bear, train.
- **Teacher Script**: "Now let's go inside the toy store! Look at all these toys! Let's name them together: a ball, a car, a doll, a robot, a teddy bear, and a train."
- **Action**: Have students repeat each word. Ask: "Which toy do you like? Raise your hand if you like the robot!"

### Slide 9: Grammar Focus (This is / That is)
- **Visual**: A child pointing to a toy close by (This) and another pointing to a toy far away (That).
- **Teacher Script**: "When a toy is CLOSE to us, we say 'This is a car.' (pick up a nearby object) When it is FAR AWAY, we say 'That is a train.' (point to something far) Let's practice!"
- **Action**: Walk around the room. Point to objects near and far. Students respond with "This is..." or "That is..."

### Slide 10: Grammar Focus (These are / Those are)
- **Visual**: A child pointing to multiple toys close by (These) and multiple toys far away (Those).
- **Teacher Script**: "What if we have TWO or THREE toys? Close: 'These are cars.' Far: 'Those are trains.' Remember: This/That = one. These/Those = many."
- **Action**: Hold up two pencils: "These are pencils." Point to books on a far shelf: "Those are books."

### Slide 11: Grammar Focus (Possessives: my / his / her)
- **Visual**: Three children each holding their favorite toy with labels: my, his, her.
- **Teacher Script**: "Look at the boy. This is HIS robot. Look at the girl. That is HER doll. What about you? Point to your pencil and say: 'This is MY pencil!'"
- **Action**: Go around the room. Point to students' items. Class says "That is his/her ___."

### Slide 12: Story Time — Corduroy Part 1, Page 1
- **Visual**: Corduroy sitting on a shelf in a big toy store. Other toys around him.
- **Text on slide**: "Corduroy lives in a big toy store. Look! This is a robot. That is a doll. These are cars. Those are balls."
- **Teacher Script**: "Now let's read a story. This is Corduroy. He is a teddy bear. He lives in a big toy store. Let's read together." (Read slowly, pointing to each toy)

### Slide 13: Story Time — Corduroy Part 1, Page 2
- **Visual**: Lisa and her mother looking at Corduroy. Lisa pointing happily.
- **Text on slide**: "Lisa comes to the store. 'Oh, Mommy! Look! There's the very bear I've always wanted.'"
- **Teacher Script**: "Look! A girl named Lisa comes to the store. She sees Corduroy. She wants him! Let's read what she says." (Read with excitement)

### Slide 14: Story Time — Corduroy Part 1, Page 3
- **Visual**: Mother pointing out that Corduroy is missing a button on his green overalls.
- **Text on slide**: "Mother says, 'Not today. He doesn't look new. He lost a button.'"
- **Teacher Script**: "Oh no! Mother says no. Why? Because Corduroy lost a button on his clothes! Look at his green overalls. Can you see? One button is missing!"

### Slide 15: Story Time — Corduroy Part 1, Page 4
- **Visual**: Corduroy looking at his overalls in surprise.
- **Text on slide**: "Corduroy is sad. 'I didn't know I lost a button. I want a button.'"
- **Teacher Script**: "Corduroy is sad. He says 'I want a button.' Poor Corduroy! Do you think Lisa will come back? We will find out tomorrow!"

### Slide 16: Activity Time — The Toy Shop Game
- **Visual**: Instructions for the Toy Shop role-play game.
- **Teacher Script**: "Now it's YOUR turn! We are going to play The Toy Shop. Half of you are shop owners. Half are customers. Shop owners: point to your toys and say 'This is my robot. That is her doll.' Customers: ask 'What is this?'"
- **Action**: Distribute toy flashcards. Students practice in pairs for 8-10 minutes. Walk around and help.

### Slide 17: Worksheet Time
- **Visual**: Preview of Worksheet 1 layout.
- **Teacher Script**: "Great job playing! Now let's do our worksheet. Please take out your pencils."
- **Action**: Distribute Worksheet 1. Guide students through each section:
  - Part A: Phonics matching (2 min)
  - Part B: Word Hunt (3 min)
  - Part C: Circle This/That/These/Those (3 min)
  - Part D: Fill in my/his/her (3 min)

### Slide 18: Wrap-up & Goodbye
- **Visual**: Corduroy waving goodbye from the shelf, looking hopeful.
- **Teacher Script**: "You did a wonderful job today! Tomorrow, we will see what happens to Corduroy and his clothes. Will his friends help him? See you tomorrow! Goodbye!"
