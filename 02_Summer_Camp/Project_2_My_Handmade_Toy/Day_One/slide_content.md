# Day One: In the Toy Store — Slide Content

Visual style: Warm watercolor storybook illustration. Soft, cozy colors (cream, warm brown, soft green, gentle gold). Hand-painted feel. Characters have gentle expressions. Background textures like aged paper. NO neon, NO dark backgrounds, NO glowing effects.

---

## Slide 1: Title
**Heading**: My Handmade Toy — Day One: In the Toy Store
**Subheading**: Project Two
**Visual description**: A charming watercolor toy store front with a warm golden glow from the windows. A small brown teddy bear (Corduroy) is visible sitting on a shelf inside. The store sign reads "TOY STORE" in a friendly hand-lettered font. Autumn leaves and a cozy atmosphere.

---

## Slide 2: Phonics Warm-up
**Heading**: Sounds Great! Long A and Long E
**Content**:
- -ai-: mail, wait, train, rain
- -ay-: day, pay, gray, crayon
- -ea-: sea, meat, beach, seat
- -ee-: feet, teeth, tree, green
**Visual description**: Four watercolor vignettes showing a train (ai), a crayon (ay), the sea (ea), and a green tree (ee). Each word is written in a friendly handwritten font below its picture.

---

## Slide 3: Vocabulary — Toys on the Shelf
**Heading**: Look at all the toys!
**Content**: ball, car, doll, robot, teddy bear, train
**Visual description**: A watercolor painting of a toy store shelf with six clearly labeled toys arranged neatly: a red ball, a blue car, a blonde doll in a dress, a silver robot, a brown teddy bear (Corduroy), and a green train. Each toy has its name written below in clear print.

---

## Slide 4: Grammar — This is / That is
**Heading**: This is close. That is far.
**Content**:
- This is a car. (pointing to something close)
- That is a train. (pointing to something far away)
**Visual description**: A watercolor classroom scene. A child holds a toy car close to their chest (labeled "This is a car."). In the background on a distant shelf, a train is visible (labeled "That is a train."). Arrows show near vs. far.

---

## Slide 5: Grammar — These are / Those are
**Heading**: These are close. Those are far.
**Content**:
- These are cars. (close, plural)
- Those are trains. (far, plural)
**Visual description**: Similar to Slide 4 but with multiple items. A child holds two toy cars (These are cars.) while pointing to three trains on a far shelf (Those are trains.).

---

## Slide 6: Grammar — My / His / Her
**Heading**: Whose toy is it?
**Content**:
- This is MY robot.
- That is HIS doll.
- That is HER ball.
**Visual description**: Three watercolor children each holding a toy. A boy proudly holds a robot (MY), another boy has a doll (HIS), and a girl has a ball (HER). Possessive words are highlighted in a warm gold color.

---

## Slide 7: Story Time — Page 1
**Heading**: Corduroy lives in a big toy store.
**Content**: Corduroy is a bear who lives in a big toy store. Day after day he waits on the shelf. Look! This is a robot. That is a doll. These are cars. Those are balls. But no one wants a small bear in green overalls.
**Visual description**: A warm watercolor painting of Corduroy (a small brown teddy bear in green overalls with one shoulder strap missing a button) sitting on a shelf surrounded by other toys. The toy store has warm wooden shelves and soft lighting. Corduroy looks a little lonely but hopeful.

---

## Slide 8: Story Time — Page 2
**Heading**: "There's the very bear I've always wanted."
**Content**: One morning, a little girl comes to the store. Her name is Lisa. She looks at Corduroy with a big smile. "Oh, Mommy! Look! There's the very bear I've always wanted."
**Visual description**: A watercolor scene of Lisa (a young girl with dark hair in a purple dress) pointing excitedly at Corduroy on the shelf. Her mother stands behind her. Lisa's eyes are bright and happy. The toy store is warm and inviting.

---

## Slide 9: Story Time — Page 3
**Heading**: "He doesn't look new. He lost a button."
**Content**: But Mother says, "Not today, dear. He doesn't look new. He lost the button to one of his shoulder straps." Corduroy watches them walk away.
**Visual description**: A watercolor close-up of Corduroy's green overalls showing one shoulder strap with a button and one without. Lisa and her mother are walking away in the background. Corduroy looks sad.

---

## Slide 10: Story Time — Page 4
**Heading**: "I didn't know I lost a button."
**Content**: Corduroy looks at his overalls. "I didn't know I lost a button," he says. "Tonight I will go and find it."
**Visual description**: A watercolor painting of Corduroy looking down at his own overalls with a surprised expression. The toy store is getting dark as evening comes. A single warm light illuminates Corduroy on his shelf.

---

## Slide 11: Let's Play — The Toy Shop Game
**Heading**: Your Turn! The Toy Shop Game
**Content**: Be the shop owner or the customer! Point to the toys and say:
- "This is my robot."
- "That is his doll."
- "These are our cars."
**Visual description**: A cheerful watercolor illustration of children playing in a pretend toy shop. One child stands behind a counter (shop owner) while another points at toys. Speech bubbles show the target sentences.

---

## Slide 12: Worksheet Time
**Heading**: Let's Write!
**Content**: Complete your worksheet. Use This / That / These / Those.
**Visual description**: A watercolor illustration of a pencil, an open worksheet, and a small Corduroy bear sitting next to it as if watching the student work.

---

## Slide 13: See You Tomorrow!
**Heading**: What will happen next?
**Content**: Tomorrow, the toys will try to help Corduroy. They want to find him new clothes! See you on Day Two!
**Visual description**: A watercolor painting of Corduroy waving goodbye from his shelf. The toy store window shows a sunset outside. Text reads "See you tomorrow!" in a warm, hand-lettered style.
