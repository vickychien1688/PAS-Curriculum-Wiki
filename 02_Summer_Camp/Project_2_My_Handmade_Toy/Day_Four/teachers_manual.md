# Day Four: My Toy Friend
## Teacher's Manual & Slide Script

**Project Topic**: My Handmade Toy
**Story Theme**: Corduroy (Part 4 — The Happy Ending)
**Time**: 85 minutes
**Target Grammar**: Review all concepts; Presentation Skills
**Target Vocabulary**: Craft materials: paper cup, rubber band, marker, tape
**Phonics**: SG5 Unit 7 (-ar-, -or-) & Unit 8 (-er-, -ir-, -ur-)
**Values**: Friendship, acceptance, sharing, hard work

---

## Slide-by-Slide Script (19 Slides)

### Slide 1: Title Slide
- **Visual**: Warm watercolor illustration of Lisa hugging Corduroy in her cozy room.
- **Teacher Script**: "Good morning, everyone! Welcome to Day Four — our FINAL day! Today we finish our story, AND you get to make your very own toy friend! Are you excited?"

### Slide 2: Phonics Introduction
- **Visual**: Title card "Sounds Great! R-Controlled Vowels" with warm watercolor border.
- **Teacher Script**: "Last phonics lesson! Today we learn about R-controlled vowels. When the letter R comes after a vowel, it changes the sound. Listen carefully!"

### Slide 3: Phonics — -ar- (SG5 Unit 7)
- **Visual**: 4 watercolor pictures in 2x2 grid: card, farm, star, car. Each word highlighted with -ar- in color.
- **Teacher Script**: "Listen: /ɑːr/ spelled A-R. Repeat: card... farm... star... car. Look up at the stars tonight! S-T-A-R."
- **Activity**: "What do you ride in? A CAR! C-A-R. Can you draw a star in the air with your finger?"

### Slide 4: Phonics — -or- (SG5 Unit 7)
- **Visual**: 4 watercolor pictures in 2x2 grid: born, horn, north, short. Each word highlighted with -or- in color.
- **Teacher Script**: "Next: /ɔːr/ spelled O-R. Repeat: born... horn... north... short. A horn goes BEEP BEEP!"
- **Activity**: Pretend to honk a horn. "Are you tall or short? I am ___!"

### Slide 5: Phonics — -er- (SG5 Unit 8)
- **Visual**: 4 watercolor pictures in 2x2 grid: ruler, dancer, teacher, water. Each word highlighted with -er- in color.
- **Teacher Script**: "/ɜːr/ spelled E-R. Repeat: ruler... dancer... teacher... water. Who is your teacher? ME! T-E-A-C-H-E-R!"
- **Activity**: Point to the teacher (yourself). "I am a teacher! You are a dancer! Show me your dance!"

### Slide 6: Phonics — -ir- (SG5 Unit 8)
- **Visual**: 4 watercolor pictures in 2x2 grid: stir, dirt, shirt, bird. Each word highlighted with -ir- in color.
- **Teacher Script**: "Same /ɜːr/ sound, spelled I-R! Repeat: stir... dirt... shirt... bird. SHIRT! We learned that word on Day Two! S-H-I-R-T."
- **Activity**: "Point to your shirt! What color is your shirt? My shirt is ___."

### Slide 7: Phonics — -ur- (SG5 Unit 8)
- **Visual**: 4 watercolor pictures in 2x2 grid: fur, burn, surf, nurse. Each word highlighted with -ur- in color.
- **Teacher Script**: "And /ɜːr/ can also be spelled U-R! Repeat: fur... burn... surf... nurse. Corduroy has soft FUR! F-U-R."
- **Activity**: "Corduroy is a bear with soft fur. Touch something soft near you!"

### Slide 8: Phonics Review Game
- **Visual**: All R-controlled vowel words in a wheel/spinner game layout.
- **Teacher Script**: "Final phonics game! I spin the wheel (point to a word). You read it and tell me: is it AR, OR, ER, IR, or UR?"
- **Activity**: Point to words randomly. Students read and identify the spelling. Celebrate correct answers enthusiastically — it's the last phonics day!

### Slide 9: Story Time — Corduroy Part 4, Page 1
- **Visual**: Lisa returning to the toy store the next morning with her piggy bank.
- **Text on slide**: "Lisa comes back. 'I'm Lisa. You are my very own bear.'"
- **Teacher Script**: "It's the next day! Look who is back — LISA! She saved her money in her piggy bank. Let's read what she says: 'I'm Lisa. You are MY very own bear.' She uses 'my'! Remember Day One?"

### Slide 10: Story Time — Corduroy Part 4, Page 2
- **Visual**: Lisa in her cozy bedroom, carefully sewing a button onto Corduroy's green overalls.
- **Text on slide**: "She takes him home. She sews a button on his green overalls."
- **Teacher Script**: "Lisa takes Corduroy home! And look — she sews a button on his green overalls! Now he looks perfect. What is he wearing? Green overalls with a button!"

### Slide 11: Story Time — Corduroy Part 4, Page 3
- **Visual**: Lisa smiling warmly at Corduroy, holding him gently.
- **Text on slide**: "'I like you the way you are,' says Lisa."
- **Teacher Script**: "This is the most beautiful sentence in the whole story. Let's read it together: 'I like you the way you are.' Lisa doesn't care that he was old or missing a button. She loves him just the way he is."

### Slide 12: Story Time — Corduroy Part 4, Page 4
- **Visual**: Corduroy and Lisa giving each other a big, warm hug.
- **Text on slide**: "'You must be a friend. I've always wanted a friend,' says Corduroy. 'Me too!' says Lisa."
- **Teacher Script**: "And here is the happy ending! Let's read Corduroy's famous last words: 'I've always wanted a friend.' And Lisa says: 'Me too!' They are best friends forever!"

### Slide 13: Value Discussion
- **Visual**: A warm picture of diverse children sharing toys and playing together.
- **Teacher Script**: "What did we learn from this story? Lisa SHARED her home with Corduroy. She accepted him the way he was. Today, YOU will make a toy friend. And we will SHARE and play together! Remember: 'I like you the way you are.'"

### Slide 14: Craft Time — Materials
- **Visual**: Clear watercolor pictures of all materials: 2 paper cups, rubber bands, scissors, markers, tape.
- **Teacher Script**: "Now it's time to make your Rubber Band Pop-Up Toy! Here is what we need: two paper cups, rubber bands, scissors, markers, and tape. Check that you have everything!"
- **Action**: Distribute materials to each student. Make sure everyone has 2 cups and at least 2 rubber bands.

### Slide 15: Craft Time — Steps (Corrected)
- **Visual**: Clear 4-step watercolor illustration showing correct procedure:
  - Step 1: Draw a face on one cup (cat/robot/monster)
  - Step 2: Cut 4 small notches on the OPEN RIM (top edge) of the decorated cup
  - Step 3: Stretch rubber bands across the OPEN RIM in an X shape, hooking into the notches
  - Step 4: Place decorated cup (rim down) on the second cup (upside-down as base). Push down and let go — JUMP!
- **Teacher Script**: "Follow these steps! Step 1: Draw a cute face. Step 2: Cut four little cuts on the TOP EDGE — the open part of the cup. Step 3: Put the rubber band across like an X. Step 4: Put it on the other cup, push down, and... let go! JUMP!"
- **Action**: Demonstrate each step slowly. Then let students work for 10-12 minutes. Walk around and help.

### Slide 16: Presentation Preparation
- **Visual**: The presentation script template on a warm chalkboard-style background.
- **Text on slide**: "Look! This is my toy. I work hard to make it. It is a _______. Let's share and play!"
- **Teacher Script**: "Before Show and Tell, let's practice what to say. Repeat after me: 'Look! This is MY toy. I work hard to make it. It is a cat. Let's share and play!'"
- **Action**: Have students practice with a partner 2-3 times. Help them fill in their animal/character name.

### Slide 17: Show and Tell
- **Visual**: A colorful "Show and Tell" banner with Corduroy cheering.
- **Teacher Script**: "It's SHOW AND TELL time! Who wants to go first? Come to the front, show us your toy, say your script, and make it JUMP!"
- **Action**: Each student comes to the front (1-2 min each). They say the script and demonstrate the jump. Class applauds after each presentation. Take photos if possible!

### Slide 18: Worksheet Time
- **Visual**: Preview of Worksheet 4 layout (draw and write).
- **Teacher Script**: "Amazing presentations! Now let's draw your beautiful toy on the worksheet and write about it."
- **Action**: Distribute Worksheet 4. Students draw their toy and complete the presentation script in writing (5-8 min).

### Slide 19: Wrap-up & Celebration
- **Visual**: A big "Great Job!" banner with Corduroy, Lisa, and all the toys celebrating together.
- **Teacher Script**: "You ALL did an AMAZING job this week! You learned new sounds, new words, read a beautiful story, and made your own toy friends! Just like Lisa said: 'I like you the way you are.' Give yourselves a BIG clap! See you in Project Three!"
- **Action**: Group photo with toys. Distribute certificates or stickers. Play celebratory music if available.
