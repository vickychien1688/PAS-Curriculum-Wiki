# Day Four: My Toy Friend — Slide Content

Visual style: Warm watercolor storybook illustration. Soft, cozy colors (cream, warm brown, soft green, gentle gold). Hand-painted feel. Characters have gentle expressions. Background textures like aged paper. NO neon, NO dark backgrounds, NO glowing effects.

---

## Slide 1: Title
**Heading**: My Handmade Toy — Day Four: My Toy Friend
**Subheading**: Project Two — Craft Day and Show and Tell!
**Visual description**: A watercolor illustration of Lisa hugging Corduroy in a cozy bedroom. A small bed for Corduroy is visible next to Lisa's bed. Craft supplies (paper cups, markers, rubber bands) are scattered on a table in the foreground, hinting at today's activity.

## Slide 2: Phonics Warm-up
**Heading**: Sounds Great! R-Controlled Vowels
**Content**:
- -ar-: card, farm, star, car
- -or-: born, horn, north, short
- -er-: ruler, dancer, bakery, soccer
- -ir-: stir, dirt, shirt, birthday
- -ur-: fur, burn, surf, purse
**Visual description**: Five watercolor vignettes showing a star and car (ar), a horn (or), a ruler (er), a shirt (ir), and a furry cat (ur). The words "car", "shirt", and "fur" are highlighted since they connect to our themes (toys, clothes, teddy bear).

## Slide 8: Craft Time — Materials
**Heading**: Let's Make a Rubber Band Pop-Up Toy!
**Content**: You need: 2 paper cups, 1 rubber band, markers, tape, stickers
**Visual description**: A watercolor still-life arrangement of craft materials on a wooden table: two white paper cups, a rubber band, colorful markers, tape, and stickers. Each item is clearly labeled with its English name.

---

## Slide 9: Craft Time — Steps
**Heading**: How to Make It (4 Steps)
**Content**:
- Step 1: Draw a face on one cup. Make it a cat, robot, or monster!
- Step 2: Cut four small lines on the top of the decorated cup.
- Step 3: Stretch the rubber band across the cuts to make an X shape.
- Step 4: Put it on the other cup. Push down and let go. JUMP!
**Visual description**: A watercolor step-by-step instruction panel with four numbered illustrations. Each step shows hands performing the action. The final panel shows the toy jumping up with a playful "JUMP!" text and motion lines.

---

## Slide 10: Show and Tell Script
**Heading**: Show and Tell — What to Say
**Content**:
"Hello! Look! This is my toy.
I work hard to make it.
It is a _______ (green robot / pink cat / blue monster).
It can jump high!
Let's share and play! Thank you!"
**Visual description**: A watercolor illustration of a child standing in front of a classroom, proudly holding up their paper cup toy. The presentation script is displayed on a warm cream-colored scroll or paper beside them.
