# Day Two: Corduroy's Clothes — Slide Content

Visual style: Warm watercolor storybook illustration. Soft, cozy colors (cream, warm brown, soft green, gentle gold). Hand-painted feel. Characters have gentle expressions. Background textures like aged paper. NO neon, NO dark backgrounds, NO glowing effects.

---

## Slide 1: Title
**Heading**: My Handmade Toy — Day Two: Corduroy's Clothes
**Subheading**: Project Two
**Visual description**: A watercolor illustration of Corduroy standing in front of a colorful wardrobe/closet full of tiny clothes. Shirts, dresses, and hats hang on small hangers. Warm, cozy bedroom atmosphere.

---

## Slide 2: Phonics Warm-up
**Heading**: Sounds Great! Long O and OI/OY
**Content**:
- -oa-: boat, coat, soap, road
- -ow-: row, bowl, pillow, window
- -oi-: oil, boil, soil, toilet
- -oy-: toy, boy, oyster, joy
**Visual description**: Four watercolor vignettes showing a boat (oa), a window with a pillow (ow), soil in a pot (oi), and a boy playing with a toy (oy). The word "toy" is highlighted with a small star since it connects to our theme.

---

## Slide 3: Vocabulary — Clothes
**Heading**: Cool Clothes!
**Content**: shirt, pants, dress, skirt, hat, shoes
**Visual description**: A watercolor clothesline strung across the slide with six items of clothing hanging from wooden pegs: a blue shirt, brown pants, a pink dress, a green skirt, a yellow hat, and red shoes. Each item has its name written below in clear handwritten font.

---

## Slide 4: Vocabulary — Adjectives
**Heading**: Big or small? New or old?
**Content**:
- big hat / small hat
- new shoes / old shoes
**Visual description**: Watercolor pairs of items showing contrast. A large floppy hat next to a tiny hat. A pair of shiny new shoes next to a pair of worn, patched old shoes. Labels clearly show the adjectives.

---

## Slide 5: Grammar — Adjective + Noun
**Heading**: The color comes first!
**Content**:
- a RED shirt (correct)
- a GREEN skirt (correct)
- a BIG hat (correct)
**Visual description**: A watercolor chalkboard showing the word order pattern: [adjective] + [noun]. Three examples with colorful illustrations next to each phrase. The adjectives are written in their actual colors (red text for "red", green text for "green").

---

## Slide 6: Grammar — What is he/she wearing?
**Heading**: What is he wearing? What is she wearing?
**Content**:
- Q: What is he wearing?
- A: He is wearing a blue shirt.
- Q: What is she wearing?
- A: She is wearing a pink dress.
**Visual description**: Two watercolor children standing side by side. A boy in a blue shirt and brown pants, and a girl in a pink dress and yellow hat. Question and answer text is displayed next to each child.

---

## Slide 7: Story Time — Page 1
**Heading**: The toys want to help Corduroy.
**Content**: It is night time in the toy store. The toys wake up! They see that Corduroy is sad. "We want to help you!" says the doll. "We will find you new clothes so you look new!"
**Visual description**: A magical watercolor night scene inside the toy store. The toys have come alive — a doll, a robot, and other toys gather around Corduroy. Moonlight streams through the store window. The atmosphere is warm and friendly despite being nighttime.

---

## Slide 8: Story Time — Page 2
**Heading**: "The doll is wearing a pink dress."
**Content**: The doll shows her clothes. "Look! I am wearing a pink dress and white shoes." The robot shows his clothes. "I am wearing a gray hat and a blue jacket." They look very nice!
**Visual description**: A watercolor painting of the doll twirling in her pink dress and white shoes, and the robot proudly showing off his gray hat and blue jacket. They are posing like fashion models for Corduroy.

---

## Slide 9: Story Time — Page 3
**Heading**: "Try this red shirt!"
**Content**: The toys find clothes for Corduroy. "Try this red shirt!" says the doll. "Try these blue pants!" says the robot. "How about this big yellow hat?" says the train.
**Visual description**: A watercolor scene of toys holding up different clothes for Corduroy to try. The doll holds a red shirt, the robot holds blue pants, and a toy train somehow balances a yellow hat. Corduroy stands in the middle looking uncertain.

---

## Slide 10: Story Time — Page 4
**Heading**: "I just want my button."
**Content**: But Corduroy shakes his head. "Thank you, friends. But I just want my button. I want my green overalls. I like them the way they are."
**Visual description**: A watercolor close-up of Corduroy gently pushing away the offered clothes, touching his green overalls lovingly. His expression is grateful but determined. The other toys look understanding.

---

## Slide 11: Let's Play — Dress Up the Bear
**Heading**: Your Turn! Dress Up the Bear
**Content**: Draw clothes on your bear! Then tell us: "My bear is wearing a _______ and _______."
**Visual description**: A watercolor illustration showing a blank bear outline template on one side, and a completed example on the other (a bear wearing a red shirt and blue hat). Colored pencils are scattered around.

---

## Slide 12: Worksheet Time
**Heading**: Let's Write About Clothes!
**Content**: Complete your worksheet. Describe what each person is wearing.
**Visual description**: A watercolor illustration of a pencil and worksheet, with Corduroy peeking over the edge of the paper, wearing his green overalls.

---

## Slide 13: See You Tomorrow!
**Heading**: Corduroy goes on an adventure!
**Content**: Corduroy still needs his button. Tomorrow night, he will go on a BIG adventure to find it! Will he find it? See you on Day Three!
**Visual description**: A watercolor painting of Corduroy looking up at a tall escalator in the department store, about to step on. The escalator disappears upward into warm golden light. Text reads "See you tomorrow!" in hand-lettered style.
