# Day Two: Corduroy's Clothes
## Teacher's Manual & Slide Script

**Project Topic**: My Handmade Toy
**Story Theme**: Corduroy (Part 2)
**Time**: 85 minutes
**Target Grammar**: Describing Nouns (Adj+Noun), Present Continuous (What is he/she wearing?)
**Target Vocabulary**: shirt, pants, dress, skirt, hat, shoes; big, small, new, old
**Phonics**: SG5 Unit 3 (-oa-, -ow-) & Unit 4 (-oi-, -oy-)

---

## Slide-by-Slide Script (18 Slides)

### Slide 1: Title Slide
- **Visual**: Warm watercolor illustration of Corduroy looking at a pile of colorful clothes.
- **Teacher Script**: "Good morning! Welcome to Day Two. Yesterday we met Corduroy in the toy store. Today we are going to look at some cool clothes! Let's find out what happens to Corduroy tonight."

### Slide 2: Phonics Introduction
- **Visual**: Title card "Sounds Great! Long O and OI/OY" with warm watercolor border.
- **Teacher Script**: "Let's warm up our voices! Today we have two new sounds: Long O and the OI/OY sound."

### Slide 3: Phonics — -oa- (SG5 Unit 3)
- **Visual**: 4 watercolor pictures in 2x2 grid: boat, coat, soap, road. Each word highlighted with -oa- in color.
- **Teacher Script**: "Listen: /oʊ/ spelled O-A. Repeat after me: boat... coat... soap... road. Can you hear the long O sound?"
- **Activity**: "What do you wear when it's cold? A coat! C-O-A-T. The /oʊ/ sound is in the middle."

### Slide 4: Phonics — -ow- (SG5 Unit 3)
- **Visual**: 4 watercolor pictures in 2x2 grid: row, bowl, pillow, window. Each word highlighted with -ow- in color.
- **Teacher Script**: "Same /oʊ/ sound, but spelled O-W! Repeat: row... bowl... pillow... window. Look at our classroom window! W-I-N-D-O-W."
- **Activity**: Point to the classroom window. "Can you see the window? Say it with me: window!"

### Slide 5: Phonics — -oi- (SG5 Unit 4)
- **Visual**: 4 watercolor pictures in 2x2 grid: oil, boil, soil, coin. Each word highlighted with -oi- in color.
- **Teacher Script**: "New sound! /ɔɪ/ spelled O-I. Repeat: oil... boil... soil... coin. This sound is like saying 'oy' but in the middle of a word."
- **Activity**: Students repeat and make a "digging" gesture for soil, a "flipping" gesture for coin.

### Slide 6: Phonics — -oy- (SG5 Unit 4)
- **Visual**: 4 watercolor pictures in 2x2 grid: toy, boy, oyster, joy. Each word highlighted with -oy- in color.
- **Teacher Script**: "Same /ɔɪ/ sound at the END of a word! Repeat: toy... boy... oyster... joy. Hey! TOY! That's our theme this week! T-O-Y!"
- **Activity**: "What are we making this week? A TOY! And who makes toys? A BOY or a girl! Both have the /ɔɪ/ sound!"

### Slide 7: Phonics Review Game
- **Visual**: All 8 phonics words mixed with pictures. Game-style layout.
- **Teacher Script**: "Game time! I say a word, you clap once if it's O-A or O-W (long O), and clap twice if it's O-I or O-Y."
- **Activity**: Say words in random order. Students clap to identify the sound pattern. Keep it fast and fun.

### Slide 8: Vocabulary Introduction (Clothes)
- **Visual**: A watercolor clothesline with clothes hanging: shirt, pants, dress, skirt, hat, shoes.
- **Teacher Script**: "Now let's learn about clothes! Look at the clothesline. Say them with me: a shirt, pants, a dress, a skirt, a hat, shoes."
- **Action**: Point to your own clothes or students' clothes as you name them. "Look! I am wearing a shirt!"

### Slide 9: Vocabulary Introduction (Adjectives)
- **Visual**: Contrasting watercolor images: big hat vs. small hat; shiny new shoe vs. dirty old shoe.
- **Teacher Script**: "Clothes can be different! This hat is BIG. This hat is SMALL. These shoes are NEW. These shoes are OLD. Repeat: big, small, new, old."
- **Action**: Hold up real objects to demonstrate big/small. Ask students: "Is your pencil big or small?"

### Slide 10: Grammar Focus (Adjective + Noun Order)
- **Visual**: Examples showing correct order: "a red shirt" (check mark) vs. "a shirt red" (X mark).
- **Teacher Script**: "In English, the describing word comes FIRST! We say 'a RED shirt', NOT 'a shirt red'. Let's practice: a green skirt, a big hat, a new dress, an old shoe."
- **Action**: Show pictures and have students say the correct phrase. Correct gently if they reverse the order.

### Slide 11: Grammar Focus (What is he/she wearing?)
- **Visual**: A boy wearing a blue shirt and a girl wearing a pink dress, with speech bubbles.
- **Teacher Script**: "When we want to ask about someone's clothes, we say: 'What is he wearing?' Answer: 'He is wearing a blue shirt.' Now you try: What is she wearing?"
- **Action**: Point to students in the class. "What is [student name] wearing?" Class answers together.

### Slide 12: Story Time — Corduroy Part 2, Page 1
- **Visual**: The toy store at night. Other toys gather around Corduroy.
- **Text on slide**: "The toys want to help Corduroy. They want him to look new."
- **Teacher Script**: "Let's continue our story! It is night time in the toy store. The toys are awake! They want to help Corduroy. Why? Because he needs to look new so Lisa will buy him!"

### Slide 13: Story Time — Corduroy Part 2, Page 2
- **Visual**: A doll and a robot showing off their clothes proudly.
- **Text on slide**: "The doll is wearing a pink dress. The robot is wearing a gray hat."
- **Teacher Script**: "Look at Corduroy's friends! What is the doll wearing? (Wait) Yes! A pink dress! What is the robot wearing? A gray hat! They look great!"

### Slide 14: Story Time — Corduroy Part 2, Page 3
- **Visual**: The toys holding up different clothes for Corduroy to try.
- **Text on slide**: "They find clothes for Corduroy. 'Try this red shirt! Try these blue pants!'"
- **Teacher Script**: "The toys find clothes for Corduroy! Let's read what they say: 'Try this red shirt! Try these blue pants!' Do you think he likes them?"

### Slide 15: Story Time — Corduroy Part 2, Page 4
- **Visual**: Corduroy shaking his head gently, pointing to his green overalls.
- **Text on slide**: "But Corduroy says, 'I just want my button. I want my green overalls.'"
- **Teacher Script**: "Does Corduroy want the red shirt? No! He loves his green overalls. He just wants his button back. 'I want MY green overalls.' What a loyal bear!"

### Slide 16: Activity Time — Dress Up the Bear
- **Visual**: Instructions for the Dress Up activity with a bear template preview.
- **Teacher Script**: "Now it's YOUR turn to be fashion designers! I will give you a bear picture. You will draw clothes on your bear. Then tell us: 'My bear is wearing a _____.'"
- **Action**: Distribute bear templates and colored pencils. Give students 10 minutes to draw. Then 5 minutes for sharing: each student holds up their bear and says one sentence.

### Slide 17: Worksheet Time
- **Visual**: Preview of Worksheet 2 layout.
- **Teacher Script**: "Beautiful bears, everyone! Now let's do our worksheet. Take out your pencils."
- **Action**: Distribute Worksheet 2. Guide students through each section:
  - Part A: Phonics matching (2 min)
  - Part B: Word Scramble (3 min)
  - Part C: Circle correct phrase (2 min)
  - Part D: Sentence Builder (4 min)

### Slide 18: Wrap-up & Goodbye
- **Visual**: Corduroy walking towards an escalator, looking determined.
- **Teacher Script**: "Great job today! Corduroy still needs his button. Tomorrow night, he goes on a BIG adventure to find it! What do you think will happen? See you tomorrow!"
