# Day Three: The Big Adventure — Slide Content

Visual style: Warm watercolor storybook illustration. Soft, cozy colors (cream, warm brown, soft green, gentle gold). Hand-painted feel. Characters have gentle expressions. Background textures like aged paper. NO neon, NO dark backgrounds, NO glowing effects.

---

## Slide 1: Title
**Heading**: My Handmade Toy — Day Three: The Big Adventure
**Subheading**: Project Two
**Visual description**: A watercolor illustration of Corduroy bravely climbing onto an escalator in a grand department store at night. The store is dimly lit with warm golden tones. Corduroy looks small but determined.

---

## Slide 2: Phonics Warm-up
**Heading**: Sounds Great! OU/OW and OO
**Content**:
- -ou-: house, mouse, cloud, count
- -ow-: cow, owl, brown, clown
- -oo- (long): moon, pool, spoon, zoo
- -oo- (short): book, cook, wood, foot
**Visual description**: Four watercolor vignettes showing a house with a mouse (ou), a brown owl (ow), the moon over a pool (oo long), and a book on a wooden shelf (oo short). The word "brown" is highlighted since Corduroy is brown!

---

## Slide 3: Grammar — Does he have...?
**Heading**: Does he have a button?
**Content**:
- Does he have a car? → Yes, he does.
- Does she have a hat? → No, she doesn't.
**Visual description**: A watercolor split scene. On the left, a boy with a toy car and a green checkmark (Yes, he does). On the right, a girl without a hat and a gentle red X (No, she doesn't). The question format is clearly displayed at the top.

---

## Slide 4: Grammar — He doesn't / She doesn't
**Heading**: He doesn't look new.
**Content**:
- He doesn't have a button.
- She doesn't want a robot.
- He doesn't look new.
**Visual description**: A watercolor illustration of Corduroy with his missing button highlighted by a small arrow. The sentence "He doesn't have a button" is written in a clear, friendly font. Other example sentences are shown below.

---

## Slide 5: Grammar Practice
**Heading**: Let's Practice! Does or Doesn't?
**Content**: Look at the pictures and answer:
1. Does the robot have a hat? (Yes, he does.)
2. Does the doll have shoes? (No, she doesn't.)
3. Does Corduroy have a button? (No, he doesn't!)
**Visual description**: Three watercolor panels showing: a robot wearing a hat, a doll without shoes, and Corduroy with his missing button. Each panel has a question below it.

---

## Slide 6: Story Time — Page 1
**Heading**: Corduroy goes up the escalator.
**Content**: Late at night, Corduroy climbs down from his shelf. He wants to find his button. He steps onto the escalator and goes UP! "Could this be a mountain?" he wonders. "I've always wanted to climb a mountain."
**Visual description**: A dramatic watercolor scene of tiny Corduroy riding up a grand escalator in the dark department store. The escalator looks enormous compared to him. Warm moonlight streams through high windows.

---

## Slide 7: Story Time — Page 2
**Heading**: "This must be a bed. I've always wanted to sleep in a bed."
**Content**: Corduroy sees tables and chairs and lamps. And rows and rows of beds! "This must be a palace!" he says. He climbs onto a big, soft bed. "I've always wanted to sleep in a bed."
**Visual description**: A watercolor painting of Corduroy standing in awe in the furniture department. Rows of beds stretch before him. He is climbing onto a large, plush bed with a quilted mattress. His eyes are wide with wonder.

---

## Slide 8: Story Time — Page 3
**Heading**: He pulls and pulls. POP!
**Content**: On the bed, Corduroy sees something small and round. "My button!" he cries. He pulls and pulls with both paws. POP! But it is not his button. It is a button on the mattress!
**Visual description**: A watercolor action scene of Corduroy tugging hard on a mattress button with both paws. The word "POP!" is written in a playful, bouncy font. The button pops off and Corduroy tumbles backward.

---

## Slide 9: Story Time — Page 4
**Heading**: Does he get the button? No, he doesn't.
**Content**: CRASH! Corduroy falls off the bed and knocks over a tall lamp. The night guard hears the noise. "Who is there?" He finds Corduroy and carries him back to the toy shelf.
**Visual description**: A watercolor scene of the night guard (a friendly-looking man with a flashlight) finding Corduroy under a blanket on the big bed. Only Corduroy's fuzzy brown ears stick out. The guard looks surprised but kind.

---

## Slide 10: Reading Comprehension
**Heading**: Do You Remember?
**Content**:
1. Does Corduroy find his button? (No, he doesn't.)
2. Does Corduroy sleep in a bed? (Yes, he does!)
3. Does the guard help Corduroy? (Yes, he does.)
**Visual description**: A watercolor "quiz" layout with three numbered questions. Small illustrations accompany each question: a button with a question mark, Corduroy on a bed, and the guard carrying Corduroy.

---

## Slide 11: Let's Play — Find the Button!
**Heading**: Find the Button Game!
**Content**: I hid a button in the classroom! Ask questions to find it: "Does the robot have the button?" "Does the doll have the button?"
**Visual description**: A watercolor illustration of a classroom with various toys placed around. A large golden button with a question mark hovers in the center. Children are shown asking questions and searching.

---

## Slide 12: Worksheet Time
**Heading**: Let's Write with Does and Doesn't!
**Content**: Complete your worksheet. Answer questions about the story.
**Visual description**: A watercolor illustration of a worksheet and pencil, with Corduroy sitting on top of a stack of books nearby.

---

## Slide 13: See You Tomorrow!
**Heading**: Will Lisa come back?
**Content**: Poor Corduroy is back on his shelf. He still doesn't have his button. But tomorrow is our last day! Will Lisa come back? And YOU will make your very own toy! See you on Day Four!
