# Day Three: The Big Adventure
## Teacher's Manual & Slide Script

**Project Topic**: My Handmade Toy
**Story Theme**: Corduroy (Part 3)
**Time**: 85 minutes
**Target Grammar**: Does Questions (Does he have...?), Negative Sentences (He doesn't...)
**Target Vocabulary**: Review toys & clothes; Story verbs: want, find, look, sleep
**Phonics**: SG5 Unit 5 (-ou-, -ow-) & Unit 6 (-oo- long/short)

---

## Slide-by-Slide Script (18 Slides)

### Slide 1: Title Slide
- **Visual**: Warm watercolor illustration of Corduroy riding an escalator in a huge department store.
- **Teacher Script**: "Good morning! Welcome to Day Three — The Big Adventure! Last night, Corduroy decided to find his button all by himself. Let's see what happens!"

### Slide 2: Phonics Introduction
- **Visual**: Title card "Sounds Great! OU/OW and OO" with warm watercolor border.
- **Teacher Script**: "Let's warm up! Today we have two sounds: the /aʊ/ sound (like 'ouch!') and the /uː/ or /ʊ/ sound."

### Slide 3: Phonics — -ou- (SG5 Unit 5)
- **Visual**: 4 watercolor pictures in 2x2 grid: house, mouse, cloud, mouth. Each word highlighted with -ou- in color.
- **Teacher Script**: "Listen: /aʊ/ spelled O-U. Repeat: house... mouse... cloud... mouth. Say 'ouch!' — that's the same sound!"
- **Activity**: "Point to your mouth! M-O-U-T-H. Can you see a cloud outside?"

### Slide 4: Phonics — -ow- (SG5 Unit 5)
- **Visual**: 4 watercolor pictures in 2x2 grid: cow, owl, brown, clown. Each word highlighted with -ow- in color.
- **Teacher Script**: "Same /aʊ/ sound, but spelled O-W! Repeat: cow... owl... brown... clown. Remember yesterday O-W said /oʊ/ like 'window'? Today O-W says /aʊ/ like 'cow'! Tricky!"
- **Activity**: "What color is Corduroy? He is BROWN! B-R-O-W-N. The /aʊ/ sound!"

### Slide 5: Phonics — -oo- long (SG5 Unit 6)
- **Visual**: 4 watercolor pictures in 2x2 grid: moon, pool, spoon, zoo. Each word highlighted with -oo- in color.
- **Teacher Script**: "New sound! /uː/ spelled O-O. Repeat: moon... pool... spoon... zoo. Look up at the moon tonight!"
- **Activity**: Make a round shape with hands for "moon." Pretend to swim for "pool."

### Slide 6: Phonics — -oo- short (SG5 Unit 6)
- **Visual**: 4 watercolor pictures in 2x2 grid: book, cook, wood, foot. Each word highlighted with -oo- in color.
- **Teacher Script**: "Same letters O-O, but a shorter sound: /ʊ/. Repeat: book... cook... wood... foot. Point to your foot! Now point to a book!"
- **Activity**: "The same letters can make two sounds! Moon = /uː/. Book = /ʊ/. Let's practice both!"

### Slide 7: Phonics Review Game
- **Visual**: All 8 phonics words mixed with pictures. Sorting game layout.
- **Teacher Script**: "Sorting game! I say a word. If it has the /aʊ/ sound (ou/ow), stand up! If it has the /uː/ or /ʊ/ sound (oo), sit down!"
- **Activity**: Say words in random order. Students stand or sit. Keep it energetic and fast.

### Slide 8: Grammar Focus (Does Questions)
- **Visual**: A picture of a boy with a red car, and a large question mark.
- **Teacher Script**: "Today we learn a new question word: DOES. When we ask about HE or SHE, we use 'Does'. 'Does he have a car?' If yes: 'Yes, he does.' Let's practice!"
- **Action**: Hold up flashcards. Ask: "Does he have a ball?" Students answer "Yes, he does" or "No, he doesn't."

### Slide 9: Grammar Focus (Negative: doesn't)
- **Visual**: A picture of a girl with a crossed-out robot (she doesn't have one).
- **Teacher Script**: "If the answer is NO, we say 'doesn't'. 'Does she have a robot? No, she doesn't. She doesn't have a robot.' Doesn't = does + not."
- **Action**: Practice with more examples using classroom objects and students.

### Slide 10: Grammar Practice (Interactive)
- **Visual**: Several characters with different toys and clothes. Question prompts.
- **Teacher Script**: "Let's practice together! Look at the pictures. I ask, you answer!"
- **Action**: Go through 4-5 questions: "Does he have a blue shirt?" "Does she have a doll?" Students answer chorally, then individually.

### Slide 11: Story Time — Corduroy Part 3, Page 1
- **Visual**: Corduroy climbing onto the escalator at night.
- **Text on slide**: "Corduroy goes up the escalator. He looks for his button."
- **Teacher Script**: "It's night time! Everyone is sleeping, but Corduroy is awake. He goes up the escalator to find his button. Let's read together."

### Slide 12: Story Time — Corduroy Part 3, Page 2
- **Visual**: Corduroy looking at a giant bed in awe, eyes wide.
- **Text on slide**: "He sees a big bed. 'This must be a bed. I've always wanted to sleep in a bed.'"
- **Teacher Script**: "Wow! Corduroy sees a big, big bed! He has never slept in a bed before. Let's read his famous words: 'I've always wanted to sleep in a bed.'"

### Slide 13: Story Time — Corduroy Part 3, Page 3
- **Visual**: Corduroy pulling hard on a button on the mattress. POP!
- **Text on slide**: "He sees a button on the bed! He pulls and pulls. POP!"
- **Teacher Script**: "Look! A button! Is it his button? He pulls and pulls... POP! Oh no! What happened? It was a button on the mattress, not his button!"

### Slide 14: Story Time — Corduroy Part 3, Page 4
- **Visual**: A security guard finding Corduroy and carrying him back to the toy department.
- **Text on slide**: "Does he get the button? No, he doesn't. The guard finds him and puts him back."
- **Teacher Script**: "Let's answer together: Does he get the button? (Wait) No, he doesn't! The guard finds him and puts him back on the shelf. Poor Corduroy!"

### Slide 15: Reading Comprehension
- **Visual**: Four True/False questions about the story with cute icons.
- **Teacher Script**: "Let's check if you remember the story! Number 1: Does Corduroy go up the escalator? (True!) Number 2: Does he find his button? (False!)"
- **Action**: Go through all questions. Have students raise hands for True, cross arms for False.

### Slide 16: Activity Time — Find the Button
- **Visual**: Instructions for the scavenger hunt game.
- **Teacher Script**: "Now we play FIND THE BUTTON! I hid paper buttons around the classroom under toy flashcards. You must ask: 'Does the robot have the button?' I will answer 'Yes, it does' or 'No, it doesn't.' The team that finds the button wins!"
- **Action**: Play 3-4 rounds. Each round, hide the button under a different flashcard. Students must use "Does... have...?" to ask.

### Slide 17: Worksheet Time
- **Visual**: Preview of Worksheet 3 layout.
- **Teacher Script**: "Great detective work! Now let's do our worksheet. Take out your pencils."
- **Action**: Distribute Worksheet 3. Guide students through each section:
  - Part A: Phonics fill-in (3 min)
  - Part B: True/False story review (3 min)
  - Part C: Circle correct answer (3 min)
  - Part D: Scrambled Sentences (4 min)

### Slide 18: Wrap-up & Goodbye
- **Visual**: Corduroy back on his shelf, looking sad but hopeful.
- **Teacher Script**: "Poor Corduroy is back on the shelf without his button. But guess what? Tomorrow is our LAST day, and something wonderful will happen! Will Lisa come back? And tomorrow, YOU will make your own toy friend! See you tomorrow!"
